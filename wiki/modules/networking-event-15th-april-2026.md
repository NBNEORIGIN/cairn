# Networking Event - 15th April 2026

This article covers the Northumberland Networking event in Wooler scheduled for Wednesday 15th April 2026, 09:30-11:00. The event is organised by Business Northumberland and will be held at The Cheviot Centre. This is part of the regular Northumberland Networking series designed to connect businesses and operators across the region.

## Event Details

**Date:** Wednesday 15th April 2026  
**Time:** 09:30-11:00  
**Duration:** 1.5 hours  
**Venue:** The Cheviot Centre, 12 Padgepool Place, Wooler  
**Organiser:** Business Northumberland Team (Northumberland County Council)

## Venue Information

### Location
The Cheviot Centre is located at 12 Padgepool Place in Wooler, Northumberland. Wooler is a small market town in North Northumberland, approximately 16 miles from Berwick-upon-Tweed.

### Parking
Free parking is available at the venue. No advance booking is required for parking spaces.

### Accessibility Considerations
When planning your journey, factor in the rural location. Check local traffic conditions and allow extra travel time, particularly if you're unfamiliar with the area.

## Pre-Event Checklist

1. **Confirm attendance** - If you haven't already done so, ensure your attendance has been confirmed with the organisers
2. **Plan your route** - Map your journey to The Cheviot Centre, allowing extra time for rural roads
3. **Prepare networking materials** - Bring business cards and any relevant information about NBNE services
4. **Review objectives** - Consider what you want to achieve from the networking event (e.g., local partnerships, community visibility, operational insights)
5. **Check communications** - Ensure you have contact details for the organiser in case of delays or queries

## On the Day

1. **Arrive early** - Aim to arrive 10-15 minutes before the 09:30 start time to allow for parking and registration
2. **Register upon arrival** - Check in with event organisers when you arrive
3. **Network actively** - Engage with other local businesses and representatives
4. **Take notes** - Record useful contacts, potential partnerships, or local intelligence relevant to NBNE operations
5. **Collect materials** - Gather any handouts, local business directories, or information shared at the event
