# Pricing Enquiries for Tourist Direction Maps

This article covers how to handle pricing enquiries for the supply and installation of tourist direction maps, using a request from Berwick-upon-Tweed Town Council as a reference example. These enquiries typically involve client-supplied artwork and require careful consideration of site-specific installation requirements.

## Initial Assessment

When receiving a pricing request for tourist direction maps:

1. **Acknowledge the enquiry promptly** - Confirm receipt and provide an estimated timeframe for the quotation
2. **Review all attached documentation** - Check for specifications including:
   - Map dimensions and quantities
   - Substrate materials required
   - Installation locations and mounting details
   - Any special finishes or anti-vandalism requirements
3. **Verify artwork specifications** - Confirm the client will supply print-ready artwork and clarify file format requirements

## Key Information to Gather

### Site Survey Requirements

Tourist direction maps installed in public spaces require thorough site assessment:

1. **Location details** - Obtain exact installation positions (GPS coordinates if available)
2. **Ground conditions** - Determine if installations are wall-mounted or free-standing
3. **Access considerations** - Identify any restrictions for installation vehicles or equipment
4. **Planning requirements** - Check if listed building consent or planning permission is needed (particularly relevant in historic towns like Berwick-upon-Tweed)

### Product Specifications

Standard tourist direction maps typically include:

- **Substrate**: Dibond, aluminium composite, or stainless steel
- **Print method**: UV-stable digital print with anti-graffiti laminate
- **Mounting**: Post-mounted lectern style or wall-mounted frames
- **Size**: Common formats are A1, A0, or custom dimensions
- **Weatherproofing**: Essential for outdoor applications

## Quotation Preparation

### Cost Components to Include

1. **Supply costs**:
   - Material and substrate
   - Printing and lamination
   - Frame or mounting hardware
   - Fixings and posts (if applicable)

2. **Installation costs**:
   - Site survey visit
   - Labour and installation time
   - Equipment hire (cherry pickers, concrete mixers, etc.)
   - Travel to location

3. **Additional considerations**:
   - Groundworks if required
   - Electrical work for illuminated options
   - Removal of existing signage
   - Waste disposal

### Pricing Structure

Present the quotation with:

- **Itemized breakdown** - Separate supply and installation costs
- **Optional extras** - Anti-vandalism features, lighting, maintenance packages
- **Validity period** - Typically 30 days
- **Payment terms** - Standard NBNE terms
- **Lead time** - From artwork approval to completion

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls

- **Assuming simple installations** - Public realm projects often involve unexpected complications (utilities, buried services, conservation areas)
- **Not clarifying artwork responsibility** - Confirm file formats, resolution requirements, and who handles final proofing
- **Overlooking planning permissions** - Council-owned locations may still require formal approvals
- **Underestimating travel costs** - Factor in distance to remote locations like Berwick-upon-Tweed
- **Missing seasonal constraints** - Ground conditions and weather may affect installation timing

## Follow-Up Actions

After submitting the quotation:

1. **Schedule follow-up** - Contact within 7-10 days if no response received
2. **Offer site visit** - Provide to discuss requirements and confirm feasibility
3. **Request artwork review** - Once order confirmed, review supplied artwork for technical suitability
4. **Confirm timelines** - Agree on production and installation schedule

## Local Authority Considerations

When dealing with town councils and local authorities:

- **Purchase order requirements** - May need formal PO before commencing work
- **Contractor requirements** - Possible need for public liability insurance certification
- **Health & safety documentation** - Risk assessments and method statements (RAMS) may be required
- **Retention periods** - Be aware of potential retention clauses in payment terms
- **Multiple stakeholders** - Decision-making may involve various council departments
