# Sign for a Shop Front

## Summary
This article covers the process of requesting and procuring an external shopfront sign from a third-party supplier. The example demonstrates a personal favour request for an office manager's daughter, including quotation requirements, specification details, and the information needed to obtain accurate pricing for ACM signage with vinyl overlam suitable for external use.

## Key Information Required for Quote

To obtain an accurate quotation for shopfront signage, suppliers require comprehensive details. Incomplete requests will result in rough estimates only.

### Essential Details

1. **Exact dimensions** - Provide measurements in metric (e.g., 2.83m x 0.27m)
2. **Material specifications** - Confirm ACM (aluminium composite) or alternative materials
3. **Artwork format** - High-resolution vector files (EPS) preferred over raster images
4. **Installation location** - Full address for delivery and installation
5. **Access considerations** - Note any restrictions, height requirements, or access challenges
6. **Site photographs** - Images showing where the sign will be mounted

### Additional Considerations

- **Fixing points** - Long, thin signs require multiple secure fixing points
- **Delivery distance** - Impacts delivery cost (supplier location: Alnwick in this case)
- **Installation complexity** - Affects installation pricing (£75-£150 range typical)

## Typical Pricing Structure

Based on the example quotation:

| Item | Specification | Cost |
|------|---------------|------|
| ACM Sign | 2.83m x 0.27m, vinyl overlam, external use | £180.00 |
| Graphic Design | 1-2 hours (if vector artwork unavailable) | £40/hour |
| Installation | Depends on complexity and access | £75-£150 |
| Delivery | Location-dependent | Variable |

**Payment Terms:** Standard terms are 25% deposit upfront, with balance due upon project completion.

## Process Steps

### 1. Initial Request
Prepare specifications including dimensions, material preferences, and colour requirements (e.g., "light cream background rather than plain white").

### 2. Artwork Submission
Submit the highest resolution design files available. Vector formats (EPS, AI, SVG) are preferred to avoid additional design costs.

### 3. Site Information
Provide:
- Delivery address
- Installation address (if different)
- Photographs of installation location
- Access considerations

### 4. Review Quotation
Expect rough estimates if information is incomplete. Formal quotes require full specifications.

### 5. Approve and Deposit
Upon accepting formal quote, arrange 25% deposit payment to commence work.

### 6. Installation
Coordinate installation timing and ensure site access is available.

### 7. Final Payment
Settle remaining balance upon project completion.

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Insufficient Artwork Resolution** - Low-resolution images (JPEG, PNG) require additional graphic design work at £40/hour. Always request vector files from original designers where possible.

⚠️ **Missing Installation Details** - Without site address and photographs, installation costs cannot be accurately quoted, potentially causing budget overruns.

⚠️ **Inadequate Fixing Points** - Long, thin signs are structurally challenging. Ensure the mounting surface can accommodate multiple fixing points, or costs may increase.

⚠️ **Delivery Distance Assumptions** - Confirm supplier location and factor delivery costs into budget, especially for remote locations.

⚠️ **Personal Favours vs Business Procurement** - This example shows a personal request via work contacts. Ensure appropriate separation between business and personal procurement to avoid conflicts of interest or policy violations.

## Professional Considerations

When handling personal requests through business channels:
- Maintain transparency with all parties
- Use personal email where appropriate
- Ensure no misuse of company resources
- Keep clear records separating business and personal transactions
- Be aware of any company policies on using business contacts for personal matters
