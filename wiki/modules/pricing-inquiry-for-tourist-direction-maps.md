# Pricing Inquiry for Tourist Direction Maps

## Summary
This article covers the process for responding to pricing inquiries for tourist direction maps, specifically for external organizations like town councils. The inquiry involves supply and installation of wayfinding signage where the customer provides artwork. This type of request requires careful consideration of site surveys, installation requirements, materials, and coordination with the client's existing artwork specifications.

## Initial Assessment

### 1. Review Customer Requirements
- **Check attached specifications** for:
  - Number of maps required
  - Dimensions and sizes
  - Material preferences (if specified)
  - Installation locations and mounting requirements
  - Any specific finish or protection requirements
- **Note:** Customer is providing artwork - confirm file format, resolution, and design specifications before quoting

### 2. Identify Key Information Gaps
Before preparing a quote, obtain:
- Exact installation locations (addresses or site plans)
- Ground conditions at each location
- Existing infrastructure (if replacing old signs)
- Access requirements for installation vehicles
- Any planning permissions already secured
- Timeline expectations

## Site Survey Requirements

### 3. Determine if Site Visit is Necessary
For Berwick-upon-Tweed or similar locations:
- Assess travel time and distance from your branch
- Consider whether photos/video call would suffice for initial assessment
- Budget site visit costs if required (may need to charge or absorb)

**⚠️ WARNING:** Never quote installation without understanding site conditions. Tourist maps in historic town centers often have underground services, conservation area restrictions, or difficult access.

### 4. Check Planning and Permissions
- Tourist signage in town centers may require:
  - Local authority planning permission (ironic when the council IS the client, but still verify internal processes)
  - Listed building consent if near historic structures
  - Highways approval if located near roads
- **Ask the client** what permissions they've already secured

## Pricing Components

### 5. Calculate Supply Costs
- **Substrate material:** Dibond, ACM, stainless steel, or other as specified
- **Size-based pricing:** Use standard pricing matrix for panel sizes
- **Print/application:** Digital print with laminate protection (outdoor rated)
- **Fixings and hardware:** Posts, frames, wall mounts as required
- **Artwork handling:** Even though client supplies artwork, include time for file checking and pre-press

### 6. Calculate Installation Costs
Break down:
- Labour hours (minimum 2-person team for safety)
- Equipment hire (post diggers, concrete, fixings)
- Travel time and mileage to Berwick-upon-Tweed
- Waste disposal and site cleanup
- Traffic management if required

### 7. Add Contingencies
- **Material waste:** 10% for outdoor installations
- **Weather delays:** Consider seasonal factors in Northumberland
- **Complications buffer:** Historic town centers often present unexpected challenges

## Quotation Preparation

### 8. Structure Your Quote
Include:
- Itemized breakdown (supply separate from installation)
- Specification of materials and finishes
- Installation methodology summary
- Exclusions (e.g., planning fees, artwork design, ground surveys)
- Payment terms
- Validity period (30-60 days standard)
- Lead time from artwork approval to installation

### 9. Terms and Conditions
Specify:
- Client responsibility for artwork supply (formats, resolution, colour profiles)
- Approval process and sign-off requirements
- Site access requirements
- Council's responsibility for permissions and consents
- Warranty terms for materials and installation

**⚠️ COMMON PITFALL:** Councils have complex procurement processes. Ask about PO requirements, quote reference numbers, and whether this needs formal tender documentation.

## Follow-Up Actions

### 10. Send Quote Promptly
- Acknowledge inquiry within 24 hours
- Provide detailed quote within 5 working days (sooner if possible)
- Include contact details for questions
- Offer site visit if they'd find it helpful

### 11. Maintain Communication
- Log inquiry in CRM system
- Set reminder for follow-up if no response within 2 weeks
- Be prepared for budget-driven negotiation (council procurement)
