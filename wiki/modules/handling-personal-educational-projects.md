# Handling Personal/Educational Projects

## Summary
This case documents a typical enquiry for a personal educational project (GCSE coursework) that ultimately did not convert to a job. The customer requested acrylic cutting services, had an initial consultation, but withdrew due to timing constraints. This type of enquiry is common and helps inform how we handle non-commercial work requests.

## Background Context

**Customer:** Vicky (parent of student Kit)  
**Project Type:** GCSE coursework requiring CNC cutting  
**Material:** 3-4mm clear acrylic  
**Files Provided:** DXF format  
**Outcome:** No sale - customer relocated before work could commence

## Key Discussion Points

### 1. Initial Enquiry Handling

The customer approached NBNE with:
- DXF files ready for cutting
- Flexible material specifications (3mm or 4mm clear acrylic)
- Physical file backup (memory stick)
- Scheduled consultation appointment

This represents good customer preparation - files in correct format, willingness to discuss materials, and advance booking.

### 2. Consultation Meeting

Toby conducted an in-person consultation where:
- Design files were reviewed
- Material options discussed
- Technical advice provided
- Timeline/logistics assessed

The customer feedback ("thanks for your time and advice") indicates the consultation was valuable even though no sale resulted.

### 3. Project Withdrawal

Customer withdrew because:
- Design adjustments needed after consultation
- Relocation to Cornwall imminent (next day)
- Timing incompatible with our production schedule

## Decision Factors: Taking Educational Projects

### ✅ When to Accept

- **Schedule Permits:** We have capacity during quieter periods
- **Simple Requirements:** Straightforward cutting jobs that don't tie up equipment
- **Learning Opportunity:** Unusual designs or techniques that benefit our skills
- **Community Goodwill:** Local schools, regular customer relationships
- **Profitable Pricing:** Even small jobs can work if priced appropriately

### ⚠️ Warning Signs

- **Tight Deadlines:** Student projects often have inflexible submission dates
- **Unclear Specifications:** "We'll work it out as we go" approaches
- **Budget Mismatches:** GCSE students may not understand commercial pricing
- **Complex Requirements:** Multi-stage work requiring significant operator time
- **File Issues:** Poorly prepared or incompatible files requiring extensive cleanup

## Common Pitfalls

### Underestimating Complexity
Educational projects can be deceptively time-consuming. Students often:
- Provide files needing significant correction
- Require multiple iterations
- Lack understanding of material constraints
- Need educational explanations (not billable time)

### Pricing Challenges
- Hard to quote competitively against school workshop equipment (free to student)
- Difficult to charge full commercial rates to students/parents
- Risk undervaluing our services if we discount too heavily
- May set unwanted precedent for pricing

### Timeline Pressures
- Coursework deadlines are inflexible
- Students often leave things until last minute
- Our commercial work must take priority
- Equipment breakdowns can't delay student submissions

## Best Practices

### 1. **Clear Communication**
- Explain lead times upfront
- Discuss material costs and minimum charges
- Set expectations about file preparation requirements
- Confirm we're a commercial operation, not a school workshop

### 2. **File Management**
- Always request DXF or compatible vector formats
- Ask for physical backup (memory stick) as well as email
- Review files before quoting - charge for cleanup if needed
- Keep copies of project files for reference

### 3. **Pricing Strategy**
- Maintain minimum job charges
- Don't undercut our commercial rates significantly
- Consider material waste and setup time
- Be transparent about costs from the start

### 4. **Scheduling**
- Only accept if we have genuine capacity
- Build in buffer time for potential issues
- Make clear that commercial work takes priority
- Get commitment deposits for material purchases

## Positive Outcomes From This Case

Even though no sale resulted:
- Customer had positive experience (good for reputation)
- Toby provided valuable advice (community goodwill)
- No time wasted on unsuitable project
- Customer withdrew promptly when circumstances changed

This is professional relationship management - not every enquiry converts, but handling them well maintains our reputation.
