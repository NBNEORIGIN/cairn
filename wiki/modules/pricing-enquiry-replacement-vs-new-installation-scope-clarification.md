# Pricing Enquiry: Replacement vs. New Installation Scope Clarification

## Summary

When quoting for signage projects involving tourist maps, wayfinding panels, or noticeboards, clients often use ambiguous terminology that doesn't distinguish between panel-only replacements and complete new installations with posts. This case demonstrates the critical step of scope clarification before pricing, where Berwick-upon-Tweed Town Council's initial enquiry for "tourist direction maps" required follow-up to determine which of three locations needed full units versus panel replacements. The client confirmed only one location (C - Parade Car Park) was attaching to an existing structure, implying the other two required new complete installations.

## Project Background

- **Client**: Berwick-upon-Tweed Town Council
- **Contact**: Stephen Robinson, Operations Manager
- **Request**: Supply and installation of tourist direction maps
- **Locations**: Three sites (A, B, and C - Parade Car Park)
- **Artwork**: Client-provided
- **Initial documentation**: Sizes and specification details attached by client

## Key Issue Identified

The purchase order descriptions did not clearly specify the installation method for each location. Without clarification, there was risk of:

- Under-quoting (pricing panel replacements when full installations were needed)
- Over-quoting (pricing complete units when only panels were required)
- Incorrect material ordering
- Installation delays due to missing posts or foundations

## Clarification Process

### Step 1: Review Initial Documentation

Examine the client's specification attachment for:
- Number of locations
- Panel dimensions
- Any mention of "replacement," "new," "existing frame," or "complete unit"
- Site photos or context clues

### Step 2: Identify Ambiguity

Look for red flags such as:
- Mixed terminology in specifications
- No explicit mention of posts or frames
- Project described only as "supply and installation" without structure details

### Step 3: Send Structured Clarification Request

Frame the question to make it easy for the client to answer:

1. List the specific question about installation scope
2. Provide clear options (full new units vs. panel replacements)
3. Ask the client to confirm for each location
4. Explain why this affects pricing

### Step 4: Document the Response

In this case, the client confirmed:
- **Location C (Parade Car Park)**: Panel attachment to existing structure
- **Locations A & B** (by implication): Require new complete installations

The client also provided a site photo to support their response.

## Quoting Implications

Based on the clarification, the quotation should include:

### For Locations A & B (New Complete Units)
- Aluminium or steel posts (specify material and finish)
- Post foundations/ground fixings
- Display frame/housing
- Printed panel with artwork
- Full installation including groundworks
- Potentially: planning permission allowance or advice

### For Location C (Panel Replacement)
- Printed panel with artwork only
- Fixings compatible with existing structure
- Installation (panel mounting only, no groundworks)
- Survey of existing frame condition (may require site visit)

## Warnings and Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Never assume "replacement" means panel-only** – some clients use this term for complete unit replacement including posts.

⚠️ **Check existing structure condition** – if panels are being fitted to existing frames, the frames may be corroded or non-standard, requiring adaptation or full replacement anyway.

⚠️ **Site photos are essential** – request images even after clarification, especially for "existing structure" installations to verify compatibility.

⚠️ **Foundation requirements vary** – new posts may require building control approval or deeper foundations in public spaces; factor this into timing and cost.

⚠️ **Client-supplied artwork** – confirm file format, resolution, and whether design fees for any adjustments should be included.

## Pricing Structure Recommendation

Create separate line items for:
1. Each new complete unit (A, B) with full installation
2. Panel replacement (C) as distinct item
3. Client-supplied artwork setup/pre-press
4. Optional extras (protective coating, anti-graffiti film, extended warranty)
5. Survey visit if required to assess location C's existing structure

## Follow-up Actions

- Request site photos for all three locations before finalizing quote
- Confirm substrate material preference (dibond, aluminium composite, etc.)
- Verify mounting height and accessibility requirements
- Check if groundworks/foundations are included or client-arranged
- Establish timescale expectations
