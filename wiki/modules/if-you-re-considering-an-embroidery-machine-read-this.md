## Decision Framework

### Key Question
**Will this machine still serve your needs in 2-3 years?**

Replacing equipment within months indicates poor initial specification. Consider:

1. **Current volume requirements**
2. **Projected growth trajectory**
3. **Product complexity** (simple logos vs. detailed designs)
4. **Turnaround time expectations**
5. **Space and workflow constraints**

### Operational Considerations

- **Entry vs. Growth:** Sunny model may be cost-effective initially but could bottleneck expanding operations
- **Sweet Spot:** MAS12 popularity suggests it handles most common use cases effectively
- **Commercial Scale:** ES-1501C and KX-T1501 represent different points on the production spectrum
- **Speed vs. Reliability:** Higher-end models emphasize both — critical for production environments

## Warnings and Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Under-specification:** Purchasing entry-level equipment that requires replacement within months wastes capital and creates workflow disruption

⚠️ **Stock availability:** Supplier indicates current stock is moving — lead times may extend if you delay

⚠️ **Price transparency:** Only Sunny model pricing is disclosed. Request full pricing and TCO (total cost of ownership) analysis before comparing options

⚠️ **Supplier lock-in:** Verify parts availability, service network coverage, and whether maintenance requires manufacturer-certified technicians
