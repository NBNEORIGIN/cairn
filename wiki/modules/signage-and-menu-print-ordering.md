# Signage and Menu Print Ordering

This article covers the process of ordering printed menus and coordinating signage installations that require electrical work. Based on a project involving A-frames, flags, lane signs with lighting, fence signs, and takeaway menus for a pizza operation.

## Summary

When ordering printed materials and signage that requires power supply, coordinate with electrical contractors early to combine installation work efficiently. Menu printing options vary significantly based on durability needs and quantity—paper/card menus are outsourced and suit larger runs (3000+), while laminated versions are produced in-house for smaller quantities and heavy use.

## Menu Print Specifications

### Choosing Print Type

**Paper/Card Menus (Outsourced)**
- Better value on larger print runs (3000+ units recommended)
- Thin card or heavy paper stock
- Suitable for: hand-to-customer, menu boxes, letterbox drops
- Requires higher minimum quantities to be cost-effective
- **Not suitable** for restaurant-style table menus requiring repeated handling

**Laminated/Durable Menus (In-house)**
- No significant price break on quantity
- Cost-effective for smaller runs
- Much more hard-wearing for repeated use
- Better for permanent fixtures or heavy-traffic environments

### Standard Specifications for Takeaway Menus

- **Size**: A5 (148mm × 210mm)
- **Recommended quantity**: 3000 for paper/card stock
- **Material**: Thin card or heavy paper weight
- **Use case**: Distribution materials, not durable table menus

⚠️ **Warning**: Don't order laminated menus if you need large quantities for letterbox drops—the cost difference is significant and unnecessary for single-use distribution.

## Coordinating Signage with Electrical Work

### When Electrical Installation Is Required

Lane signs and other outdoor signage often require lighting for evening visibility. This creates an opportunity to coordinate multiple electrical installations.

### Coordination Process

1. **Identify all electrical needs** before contacting contractors:
   - Sign lighting requirements
   - CCTV installations
   - Any other power supply needs on the same route

2. **Contact electrical contractor ("sparky")** to discuss:
   - Combined cable runs to reduce installation costs
   - Safe routing across lanes or other obstacles
   - Power supply options for multiple installations

3. **Communicate back to signage supplier** once electrical plan is confirmed:
   - Confirm cable routing is feasible
   - Finalize lighting specifications
   - Coordinate installation timing

### Recommended Lighting for Lane Signs

- **Type**: Mains-powered LED flood light
- **Benefits**: Cost-effective, reliable, good visibility
- **Requirement**: Safe cable run across the lane
- **Installation**: Signage supplier can arrange if needed

⚠️ **Warning**: Do not finalize lane sign lighting options until you've confirmed with your electrical contractor that a cable run is feasible and safe. Retrofitting lighting after installation is significantly more expensive.

## Combining Installation Projects

### Cost Benefits

When multiple installations require the same contractor or infrastructure:
- Cable runs can be combined
- Single site visit instead of multiple
- Reduced overall labor costs
- Coordinated project management

### Example Combinations

- Sign lighting + CCTV installation
- Multiple fence-mounted signs installed together
- Boundary signs and lane signs in single visit

### Project Management Steps

1. List all pending electrical/installation work
2. Identify which items can share infrastructure (cable runs, mounting points)
3. Contact contractors to discuss combined approach
4. Get single comprehensive quote rather than multiple small ones
5. Confirm scope with all suppliers (signage, electrical, etc.)
6. Schedule single installation window

## Common Pitfalls

❌ **Ordering wrong menu type**: Don't order expensive laminated menus for letterbox distribution

❌ **Separate installations**: Installing sign first then trying to add lighting later requires additional site visits and costs

❌ **Wrong print quantities**: Ordering small runs of paper/card menus loses economy of scale benefits

❌ **Missing size specifications**: Always provide approximate sizes for custom fence signs before quoting

❌ **Not considering exposure**: Standard flags weather quickly in exposed locations—consider durable options for rural/coastal sites
