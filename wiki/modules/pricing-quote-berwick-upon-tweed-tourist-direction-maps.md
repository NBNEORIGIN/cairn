## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Mixed scope projects** - Always clarify new installations vs. replacements upfront. Pricing a full unit when customer expects panel replacement creates quote rejection and wastes time.

⚠️ **Existing structure compatibility** - For Location C, conduct site survey before confirming pricing. Existing frame may require modification or may not accommodate proposed panel dimensions.

⚠️ **Customer-supplied artwork** - Establish clear specifications for file format, resolution, colour profile, and bleed. Poor artwork quality leads to delays and disputes.

⚠️ **Remote location factors** - Berwick-upon-Tweed is in Northumberland near Scottish border. Factor travel time and accommodation for installation team if multi-day job.

⚠️ **Council procurement** - Town councils often have specific procurement requirements, PO processes, and payment terms. Clarify upfront.

## Quoting Best Practices

- **Itemize clearly** - Separate pricing for Location C versus new units prevents confusion
- **Include exclusions** - State what's not included (e.g., planning permissions, utility diversions)
- **Site survey** - Always recommend pre-installation survey for existing structure work
- **Payment terms** - Council customers may require net 30 or specific invoice formats
- **Validity period** - State quote validity (typically 30 days)
