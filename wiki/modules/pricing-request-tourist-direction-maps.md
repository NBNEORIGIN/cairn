# Pricing Request: Tourist Direction Maps

## Summary
This article covers the process for handling pricing requests for tourist direction maps, specifically for external clients like town councils. The request involves supply and installation of wayfinding signage where the client provides artwork. This type of enquiry requires careful scoping of specifications, site assessment considerations, and coordination between sales, design review, and installation teams.

## Request Details

**Client:** Berwick-upon-Tweed Town Council  
**Contact:** Stephen Robinson, Operations Manager  
**Request Type:** Supply and installation of tourist direction maps  
**Key Detail:** Customer is providing their own artwork

## Processing Steps

### 1. Review Attached Specifications
- Check the attachment for exact dimensions, quantities, and material specifications
- Note any specific mounting requirements or finish details
- Verify if multiple map locations are required throughout Berwick-upon-Tweed
- Document all requirements in Cairn quote record

### 2. Clarify Artwork Specifications
Since the client is supplying artwork, confirm:
- File format and resolution requirements (vector files preferred)
- Colour specifications (CMYK vs Pantone)
- Whether artwork is print-ready or requires design review
- Responsibility for proofing and sign-off process
- Any branding guidelines to follow

### 3. Site Assessment Requirements
Tourist direction maps typically require consideration of:
- **Location type:** Street furniture, wall-mounted, post-mounted
- **Ground conditions:** For installation (concrete, paving, soil)
- **Planning permissions:** May be required for town centre installations
- **Existing infrastructure:** Utility locations, street furniture conflicts
- **Accessibility compliance:** Height and positioning requirements

> **WARNING:** Always establish whether planning permission or highways consent is required for tourist signage in town centres. Local authority clients may assume this is handled, but installation teams need confirmation before proceeding.

### 4. Quotation Components
Break down pricing to include:
- **Materials:** Sign panels, substrate type, protective coating
- **Manufacturing:** Printing, fabrication, finishing
- **Hardware:** Posts, mounting brackets, fixings
- **Installation:** Labour, equipment hire, groundworks if required
- **Site preparation:** Any necessary foundation work
- **Project management:** Design review, liaison time
- **Delivery:** Transport to Berwick-upon-Tweed (factor in distance from workshop)

### 5. Information to Request
Follow up with Stephen Robinson for:
- Proposed installation locations (addresses or map references)
- Access restrictions or working hour limitations
- Timeline/deadline requirements
- Budget parameters if available
- Contact for site visit coordination
- Whether this is part of a broader wayfinding scheme

### 6. Site Visit Considerations
Given the location in Berwick-upon-Tweed:
- Factor travel time and costs into quotation
- Combine with site survey for accurate installation pricing
- Take photographs of proposed locations
- Measure existing infrastructure
- Check for underground services (utility plans required)
- Assess equipment access for installation

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Customer-supplied artwork issues:** Artwork provided by clients often requires modification or reformatting. Build in time for design review and potential revision cycles.

⚠️ **Installation complexity:** Tourist maps in historic town centres may have restrictions on fixings, ground disturbance, or aesthetics. Berwick-upon-Tweed has heritage considerations.

⚠️ **Timescales:** Town councils often have budget year deadlines. Clarify any time constraints early.

⚠️ **Approval processes:** Local authority projects may require multiple stakeholder approvals. Factor extended timelines into project planning.

## Quotation Validity and Terms

- Standard quotation validity: 30 days
- Payment terms for local authority clients typically 30 days from invoice
- Consider stage payments for larger multi-location projects
- Include provisional sums for any unknown groundworks

## Next Actions

1. Acknowledge receipt of enquiry (same day)
2. Review attachment and document specifications
3. Send information request to client
4. Arrange site visit if required for accurate pricing
5. Coordinate with production team on lead times
6. Prepare detailed quotation within 5-7 working days
