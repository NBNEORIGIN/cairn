# Ink Supply Management and Ordering

This article covers ink cartridge monitoring, ordering procedures, and supplier account setup for NBNE's Mutoh printer system. Proper ink management prevents production downtime and ensures cartridges are ordered before critical levels are reached, while maintaining cost-effective inventory practices.

## Ink Level Monitoring

### Critical Thresholds

Monitor ink levels daily through the printer control panel or software interface. Establish clear reorder points to prevent running out during production:

- **URGENT (0-35%)**: Order immediately - risk of running out during active jobs
- **LOW (36-50%)**: Order within 48 hours - normal lead time buffer
- **ADEQUATE (51-75%)**: Monitor weekly - order if approaching busy period
- **GOOD (76-100%)**: No immediate action required

**WARNING**: Black and cyan inks typically deplete fastest due to common usage in composite colours and text. Never let these drop below 30% without confirmed replacement stock.

### Stock Cartridge Expiry

Expired ink cartridges can cause:
- Print quality degradation (banding, colour shifts)
- Printhead clogging and damage
- Warranty issues if printer damage occurs
- Wasted substrate from failed prints

**Best Practice**: Implement First-In-First-Out (FIFO) rotation for spare cartridges. Label all stock with receipt date and expiry date clearly visible. Check expiry dates monthly and prioritise using cartridges approaching expiration.

## Ordering Process

### 1. Supplier Account Setup

Contact your ink supplier to establish a credit account:
- Request account application forms
- Complete forms with company details (legal name, VAT number, trading address)
- Provide two trade references if requested
- Submit director information for credit checks
- Typical setup time: 5-10 business days

Benefits of credit accounts include consolidated monthly invoicing, improved cash flow management, and often preferential pricing.

### 2. Mutoh Ink Ordering

For genuine Mutoh inks (recommended for warranty compliance):

**Initial Order Request**:
- Contact supplier with printer model specification
- Request itemised pricing for each colour required
- Confirm cartridge capacity (typically 220ml, 440ml, or 1L)
- Ask about bulk discounts for multiple cartridges

**Order Quantities Based on Current Levels**:
- Black at 36%: Order 2 cartridges (high usage colour)
- Cyan at 29%: Order 2 cartridges (high usage colour)
- Magenta at 24%: Order 1 cartridge (order 2 if busy period approaching)
- Yellow at 57%: Monitor only (order when reaches 45%)
- Varnish at 66%: No action required
- White at 9%: **CRITICAL** - order 1-2 cartridges immediately if white printing is used

### 3. Standard Reorder Template

Maintain a reorder template with:
- Printer model and serial number
- Ink type/specification codes for each colour
- Standard order quantities
- Supplier contact details and account number
- Expected lead times (typically 2-5 days for stock items, 1-2 weeks for special orders)

## Cost Management

### Service Contract Considerations

If quoted service call-out charges are prohibitive (e.g., £500 per visit):

**Alternative Options**:
- Request itemised annual service contract pricing
- Negotiate reduced call-out fees with regular maintenance schedule
- Explore time-and-materials pricing without call-out fees
- Compare multiple service providers
- Consider manufacturer-direct service agreements

**Calculate Break-Even**: If you require more than 2-3 service visits annually, a service contract typically becomes cost-effective even at higher upfront costs.

### Ink Cost Optimisation

- Order in bulk when cash flow permits (cartridges have 12-18 month shelf life)
- Compare genuine Mutoh vs third-party inks (consider warranty implications)
- Track ink consumption per job type to improve estimating
- Implement colour profiles that reduce ink usage without quality loss

## Common Pitfalls

**Never mix ink batches or brands within the same colour** - this can cause chemical reactions, clogs, and printhead damage.

**Don't ignore low ink warnings** - running a cartridge completely dry introduces air into the ink system, requiring expensive purging procedures.

**Expired cartridges aren't just ineffective** - they can permanently damage printheads costing thousands to replace.

**Plan for lead times** - special orders may take 1-2 weeks; don't assume next-day availability.
