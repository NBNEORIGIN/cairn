# Siemens SFS Contract Support - Automated Email Responses

When submitting support tickets to Siemens for SFS (Siemens Fire Safety) contracts, you will receive an automated acknowledgement email confirming receipt of your enquiry. This article explains what to expect from these automated responses and how to effectively manage your support requests with Siemens Customer Services.

## Understanding the Automated Response

Siemens Customer Services sends automated acknowledgement emails for all support tickets raised under SFS contracts. These emails serve to:

1. Confirm your enquiry has been successfully logged in their system
2. Provide you with a unique ticket number for tracking
3. Set expectations for response times
4. Offer alternative contact methods for urgent issues

## Key Information from the Automated Response

### Response Time Expectations

- **Standard Response Time**: Up to 5 working days
- **Reason**: High volume of messages
- This is acknowledgement only - not resolution time

### Your Ticket Number

The ticket number appears in the email subject line (e.g., "Ticket Number 1675481"). **Always retain this number** for:

- Tracking your enquiry status
- Adding additional information to the same ticket
- Calling Customer Services about the issue
- Internal NBNE logging and documentation

### Adding Further Details

If you need to provide additional information after submitting your initial enquiry:

1. Reply directly to the automated response email
2. Include your ticket number in the subject line (usually already present)
3. Your additional details will be appended to the existing ticket
4. **Do not create a new email thread** - this may generate a duplicate ticket

## Escalation for Urgent Matters

### When to Escalate

If your issue requires immediate attention due to:

- Life safety systems being compromised
- Complete system failures
- Regulatory compliance deadlines
- Emergency situations affecting building occupants

### How to Escalate

1. **Call**: 01753 980078
2. **Have ready**:
   - Your ticket number from the email subject line
   - Contract number (e.g., A10133672 S17)
   - Site details and fault description
   - Any relevant P number or site identifier
3. **Quote** your ticket number immediately to link the call to your email

> **Warning**: Do not wait for email response if the issue is genuinely urgent. The 5-day response time is for standard enquiries only.

## Best Practices

### When Raising Tickets

1. **Use clear subject lines** that include:
   - Contract number
   - Site reference
   - Brief description of issue

2. **Include in your initial email**:
   - Full site address
   - Specific equipment/panel affected
   - Fault symptoms or issue description
   - Contact details for site access
   - Preferred resolution timeframe

3. **Keep the email thread intact** for continuity

### Internal NBNE Documentation

1. Log the Siemens ticket number in your NBNE job records
2. Note the date of submission
3. Set a reminder to follow up if no response within 5 working days
4. Document any phone escalations separately

## Common Pitfalls

> **Pitfall 1**: Creating multiple tickets for the same issue by sending separate emails instead of replying to the original thread. This fragments communication and delays resolution.

> **Pitfall 2**: Assuming the automated response means someone is actively working on your ticket. It is only an acknowledgement - actual work begins when Customer Services reviews your enquiry.

> **Pitfall 3**: Not escalating genuinely urgent issues by phone. The email channel is not monitored for emergency response.

> **Pitfall 4**: Losing track of ticket numbers. Without this reference, Siemens cannot easily locate your enquiry, especially when calling.

## Reference Code

The code at the bottom of Siemens emails (e.g., [GW26Y4-R39P3]) is an internal Siemens reference. You typically don't need this, but keep the email on file in case Siemens requests it.
