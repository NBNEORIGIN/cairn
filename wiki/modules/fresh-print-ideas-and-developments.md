# Fresh Print Ideas and Developments

This article covers four key printing technology developments from Roland DG that NBNE operators should be aware of when considering service expansion, workflow improvements, or bringing more work in-house. These include direct-to-object UV printing, integrated print-and-cut solutions, direct-to-film (DTF) transfer technology, and design workflow software.

## 1. Direct-to-Object UV Printing (MO Series)

The Roland DG MO Series enables direct UV printing onto three-dimensional objects up to 204mm in diameter.

**Supported Materials:**
- Plastic
- Acrylic
- Wood
- Metal
- Glass
- Cardboard
- Leather

**Use Cases:**
- Custom promotional items
- Product branding
- Prototype marking
- Personalised merchandise
- Industrial part identification

**Considerations:**
This technology allows you to take on work that would traditionally require outsourcing or alternative decoration methods like vinyl wrapping or screen printing. Evaluate whether your customer base has demand for cylindrical or curved object printing before investing.

## 2. Print-and-Cut Integration (TrueVIS XG-640)

The TrueVIS XG-640 combines printing and contour cutting in a single automated process.

**Key Specifications:**
- Production speed: up to 15.2 m²/h
- Single-device workflow eliminates manual transfer between machines
- Suitable for signage, decals, labels, and vehicle graphics

**Operational Benefits:**
- Reduces handling time between print and cut stages
- Minimises registration errors
- Decreases floor space requirements compared to separate printer and cutter
- Improves job turnaround time

**Common Pitfalls:**
- Ensure proper material loading tension to maintain registration accuracy over long runs
- Account for the single-point-of-failure risk: if the machine is down, both print and cut operations stop
- Plan maintenance windows carefully as they affect both capabilities simultaneously

## 3. Direct-to-Film (DTF) Technology (TY-300i)

The TY-300i is a DTF transfer printer that received the 2026 BLI Pick Award for durability and performance.

**What is DTF:**
DTF prints designs onto a special film with adhesive powder, which is then heat-pressed onto fabric. Unlike DTG (direct-to-garment), DTF works on a wider range of fabrics and colours without pre-treatment.

**Validated Performance:**
- Durability tested beyond 20 wash-and-dry cycles
- Independent verification by BLI (Buyers Lab Inc.)
- Strong speed and print quality ratings

**Applications:**
- Custom apparel production
- Small-to-medium run garment decoration
- Multi-fabric applications
- On-demand merchandise

**Advantages over alternatives:**
- No fabric pre-treatment required
- Works on dark and light fabrics with same process
- Can be applied to polyester, cotton, blends, and even some non-textile materials
- Lower setup costs than screen printing for short runs

**Warning:**
DTF requires specific consumables (film, powder, adhesive) and heat press equipment. Factor ongoing consumable costs into job pricing. Storage conditions for film and powder affect print quality.

## 4. Design Workflow Software (Roland DG Connect Designer)

Roland DG Connect Designer is file preparation software designed to streamline the transition from design to production.

**Key Functions:**
- Simplified file preparation for print production
- Error reduction in pre-production stage
- Faster job processing
- Reduces dependency on external design support or specialised operators

**Operational Impact:**
This tool addresses a common bottleneck in print operations: the time spent correcting, reformatting, or preparing customer-supplied files. By standardising and simplifying this process, you can:
- Take on more jobs with existing staff
- Reduce turnaround time
- Decrease rework from file-related errors
- Enable less specialised staff to handle file prep

**Integration Considerations:**
Evaluate how this software fits with your existing workflow, RIP software, and colour management systems. Test with your typical file types before full deployment.

## Strategic Considerations for Service Expansion

When evaluating these technologies, consider:

1. **Customer demand analysis**: Survey existing customers and local market for potential applications
2. **Investment prioritisation**: Which technology addresses your biggest current limitation or opportunity?
3. **Training requirements**: Factor in operator training time and learning curves
4. **Consumable costs**: Ongoing operational costs can significantly impact profitability
5. **Maintenance planning**: Understand service requirements and downtime implications
