# Pricing Enquiry: Berwick-upon-Tweed Tourist Direction Maps

## Summary

This article documents the pricing enquiry process for Berwick-upon-Tweed Town Council's tourist direction map project (April 2026). The project involves multiple locations with a mix of panel replacements and new installations. The key clarification established was that only **Location C - Parade Car Park** requires attachment to an existing structure, while other locations require full new units. This case demonstrates the importance of early scope clarification to ensure accurate quoting and avoid costly misunderstandings.

## Project Background

**Customer:** Berwick-upon-Tweed Town Council  
**Contact:** Stephen Robinson (Operations Manager)  
**Project Type:** Tourist direction maps - supply and installation  
**Artwork:** Customer-supplied  
**Key Requirement:** Mixed scope (new units + panel replacements)

## Enquiry Process

### 1. Initial Contact (10/04/2026 11:55)

- Customer requested pricing for tourist direction maps
- Artwork to be provided by the council
- Specifications and sizes attached by customer
- Multiple locations identified in attached documentation

### 2. Scope Clarification Request (10/04/2026 15:00)

**Critical Step:** Before proceeding with pricing, Toby (Sales) identified potential ambiguity in the scope:

- Project appeared to include both replacement panels AND new complete units
- Requested specific confirmation on which locations required:
  - Full new units (frame + posts)
  - Panel replacements into existing structures

**Why This Matters:**
- Full units vs. panel replacements have significantly different material costs
- Installation methods and labour requirements differ substantially
- Posts/groundwork may require additional surveys or permissions
- Accurate quoting prevents budget overruns and customer disputes

### 3. Customer Response (Same Day)

Stephen Robinson clarified:
- **Only Location C (Parade Car Park)** attaches to existing structure
- All other locations by implication require new installations
- Image of the area provided for reference

## Key Takeaways for Operators

### ⚠️ Common Pitfall: Assuming Scope

**Never assume** whether a project requires new posts or panel replacements. The difference in cost and installation complexity is substantial:

- **New units:** Require groundwork, concrete, posts, frames, and panels
- **Panel replacements:** Typically require only new printed panels and minimal installation time
- **Existing structure attachment:** May require structural assessment and specific fixing methods

### Best Practice Checklist

When handling similar enquiries:

1. ☑️ **Review all attachments thoroughly** before responding
2. ☑️ **Identify ambiguities early** - don't proceed with incomplete information
3. ☑️ **Ask specific questions** about each location if multiple sites involved
4. ☑️ **Request site photographs** where helpful (as provided in this case)
5. ☑️ **Confirm substrate/attachment method** for existing structure installations
6. ☑️ **Clarify artwork supply format** and responsibilities
7. ☑️ **Document all clarifications** in writing

### Pricing Considerations

Once scope is confirmed, factor in:

- **Location C (Parade Car Park):** 
  - Structural assessment of existing structure
  - Appropriate fixings for attachment method
  - Access requirements for installation
  
- **Other Locations:**
  - Full post and frame supply
  - Ground conditions survey
  - Concrete footings/installation
  - Potential planning permissions

### Site-Specific Notes: Berwick-upon-Tweed

- Historic town location - may have heritage considerations
- Coastal location - specify marine-grade materials where appropriate
- Multiple public locations - consider access restrictions and timing
- Council customer - may require specific procurement procedures

## Installation Planning

For projects with mixed scope:

1. **Schedule efficiently:** Group similar work types
2. **Material ordering:** Separate BOMs for replacements vs. new units
3. **Risk assessment:** Different hazards for groundwork vs. panel replacement
4. **Resource allocation:** May require different skill sets/teams
