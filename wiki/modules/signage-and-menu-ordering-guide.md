# Signage and Menu Ordering Guide

This article covers the practical process of ordering signage and printed materials for NBNE sites, using a real signage procurement example. It demonstrates how to work with suppliers to balance quality, durability and cost-effectiveness, including decision-making around material choices, quantities, and phased procurement approaches.

## Overview

When ordering signage and promotional materials for a site, you'll typically need to:

1. Gather initial quotes for all required items
2. Prioritise items based on budget and immediate operational needs
3. Make material choice decisions (wood vs plastic vs aluminium, standard vs heavy-duty)
4. Consider cost-saving alternatives where appropriate
5. Place orders in stages if necessary

## Key Decision Points

### Material Selection for A-Frame Signs

**Wood A-frames** are the standard option at £215 + VAT each. However, wood rots in outdoor environments.

**Alternatives:**
- **Plastic wood-effect A-frames**: More expensive than standard wood but more durable. Requires custom fabrication of panels on top of frame cost.
- **Aluminium A-frames with powder coating**: Can be fabricated and powder coated (e.g., green). Request pricing if durability is a priority.

**Decision criteria**: If the frames are heavy enough in plastic to remain stable, this is preferable to wood. Otherwise, stick with wood for cost reasons.

### Flags vs Banner Poles

Standard outdoor-rated flags at 4150mm × 910mm cost £450 + VAT each (includes aluminium poles and ground spikes). This is significantly more expensive than promotional flags due to durability requirements in exposed locations.

**Cost-saving alternatives:**
- Reduce flag size
- Reduce quantity
- **Use banner poles instead**: Can be secured to trees or fence posts, significantly cheaper than flag systems

⚠️ **Warning**: Cheap promotional flags are not suitable for exposed outdoor locations. They will not last.

### Menu Printing Quantities

For quantities of 3,000 menus, outsourcing to a trade press printer is more cost-effective than in-house printing. Standard specification:
- A5 size
- Full colour
- Heavy paper / thin card stock
- Suitable for handouts, menu boxes, and letterbox drops
- Cost: £295 + VAT for 3,000 units

## Phased Procurement Approach

When budget is constrained, prioritise items and order in stages:

**Example priority order:**
1. Menus (immediate need for customer-facing materials)
2. A-frame signs (directional signage)
3. Lane signs (roadside visibility)
4. Flags or banner poles (visual branding)
5. Additional fence-mounted signage

This approach allows you to get critical items in place quickly while planning for remaining elements.

## Standard Specifications

### Lane Signs (Roadside)
- Size: 8' × 2' (sponsor-board style)
- Material: ACM panel with medium channel rail
- Fixings: VHB tape and butterfly clips
- Cost: £295 + VAT
- **Recommendation**: Add mains-powered lighting if possible for visibility

### Fence-Mounted Signs
- Standard size: 900 × 600 mm
- Material: ACM panels
- Fixings: Screw fixings only
- Cost: £95 + VAT each

⚠️ **Note**: Always confirm exact sizing requirements before finalising quotes for custom fence signage.

## Working with Suppliers

### Information to Provide
- Exact quantities needed
- Preferred materials (or ask for alternatives)
- Size specifications for all custom items
- Timeline requirements ("asap" vs specific dates)

### What to Expect from Supplier
- Proofs supplied before production for all items
- Alternative options when costs are high
- Breakdown of costs (especially for composite items like A-frames where frame + panels are separate)
- Recommendations on durability and suitability for location

### Quote Review Process
1. Review total cost against budget
2. Identify high-cost items that could be substituted or reduced
3. Ask for alternative pricing on specific items
4. Prioritise essential items if ordering in stages
5. Confirm all specifications before proceeding

## Common Pitfalls

❌ **Underestimating outdoor durability requirements**: Exposed locations require heavy-duty materials. Cheap alternatives won't last.

❌ **Not requesting material alternatives upfront**: Suppliers may not volunteer better options unless asked.

❌ **Ordering everything at once without priorities**: This can blow the budget. Stage your orders.

❌ **Forgetting to confirm custom dimensions**: Always double-check measurements for fence-mounted and custom signage before production begins.

❌ **Overlooking lighting for roadside signs**: Illumination makes a significant difference to visibility and effectiveness.

## Cost Planning

- 3× A-frame signs: £645 + VAT

- 1× lane sign: £295 + VAT
- 2× fence signs: £190 + VAT
- 3,000 menus: £295 + VAT
- **Total range**: £1,825 - £2,325 + VAT (before additional custom items)
