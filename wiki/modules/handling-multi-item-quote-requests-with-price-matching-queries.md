# Handling Multi-Item Quote Requests with Price Matching Queries

When a customer requests quotes for multiple items and references previous pricing they've received, it's important to maintain pricing consistency while offering clear product alternatives. This article covers how to handle situations where clients ask for minimum quotes on multiple items and mention earlier pricing from colleagues or previous enquiries.

## Understanding the Request

The customer (Amit) is asking for:

1. **Minimum quotes for each item** – not necessarily the cheapest product, but the lowest price option for each specified product type
2. **Reference to previous pricing** – comparing current quote to one received "a couple of months ago"
3. **Simplified presentation** – wants to show one clear option per item to his client

## Key Response Steps

### 1. Check Previous Quote History

Always verify the earlier quote referenced:
- Search by customer email, phone, or company name
- Check the date of the previous quote (pricing may have changed legitimately)
- Note any differences in specification, quantity, or delivery

**Warning:** Don't dismiss the customer's claim without checking. Even if you don't find the quote, acknowledge their reference and explain current pricing factors.

### 2. Identify What Changed

In this case, Toby correctly noted:
- Current pricing reflects present material and production costs
- Limited scope for adjustment
- The previous quote was for **different quantities** (50 cards vs. 110 cards requested)
- The previous quote **didn't include** desktop pull-up banners

### 3. Offer Strategic Alternatives

Rather than refusing or defending the quote, Toby provided:

**Cost-effective option:**
- 2 × A1 PVC banners with eyelets: £50.00 + VAT (matches previous pricing)
- 50 × A6 thank you cards: £25.00 + VAT (matches previous pricing)

**Premium option:**
- Roller banners at £140.00 + VAT for 2 units

This approach:
- Shows flexibility where possible
- Maintains margin on core products
- Gives customer choice to present to their client

### 4. Address the "Minimum Quote" Request

When a customer asks for "minimum quotes," they typically mean one of three things:

1. **Lowest price for each specified product** – select the cheapest variant that meets their spec
2. **Fewest options to choose from** – one clear recommendation per item, not multiple alternatives
3. **Minimum viable solution** – the most economical way to achieve their goal

**Amit's request is type 2** – he wants one option per item to show his client, not a range of choices.

## How to Structure the Response

### Do:
- Acknowledge the previous pricing reference professionally
- Explain briefly why pricing differs (materials, specs, quantities) without being defensive
- Highlight which items from the current quote match previous pricing
- Offer a clear recommendation for items where pricing increased
- Keep the tone solution-focused

### Don't:
- Ignore the previous quote mentioned
- Provide extensive justification for pricing (brief is better)
- Offer multiple alternatives again if they've asked for one option
- Assume bad intent – they may genuinely need budget approval

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Pitfall 1: Over-explaining price increases**  
Brief acknowledgment is better than lengthy justification. "Our current pricing reflects present material and production costs" is sufficient.

⚠️ **Pitfall 2: Not checking specification differences**  
The original request was for 110 cards; previous quote was 50 cards. Always note these discrepancies.

⚠️ **Pitfall 3: Offering too many options when "minimum" is requested**  
If they ask for simplified options, give one clear path forward per item.

⚠️ **Pitfall 4: Matching old pricing without checking margin**  
Toby maintained the £50 and £25 pricing on items where possible, but didn't discount items where costs had genuinely increased.

## Next Steps Template

When providing the simplified quote:

1. List each item with **one** recommended option
2. Include individual prices and total
3. Confirm delivery address and timeline
4. State clearly: "If you'd like to proceed with any or all items, just confirm and I'll send the payment link"
5. Offer availability for questions
