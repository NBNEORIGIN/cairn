# Poster Design Approval Process

This article documents the typical workflow for poster design projects, from initial concept presentation to client approval. The process demonstrates how we present multiple design options to clients, gather feedback, and confirm final design selections before proceeding to production. This case study shows a successful design approval with clear client communication and holiday scheduling considerations.

## Process Overview

### 1. Initial Design Presentation

When presenting poster design options to clients:

- **Create multiple concepts**: Present 2-3 design variations to give clients meaningful choice without overwhelming them
- **Number your options clearly**: Use consistent numbering (e.g., "Design 1", "Design 2") to make client feedback unambiguous
- **Set appropriate timeline**: Send designs with sufficient time for review, considering upcoming holidays or client availability
- **Professional sign-off**: Use standard business email formatting with clear contact details

### 2. Client Review Period

After sending design options:

- **Allow adequate response time**: Clients may need 1-3 business days to review and consult with stakeholders
- **Factor in out-of-office periods**: Be aware of upcoming holidays or vacation periods that may delay responses
- **Monitor email for responses**: Check both standard inbox and any mobile/remote systems clients might use

### 3. Design Approval

When receiving client feedback:

- **Confirm specific selection**: Note exactly which design number/option the client has approved
- **Document approval**: Save the approval email in the project file for production reference
- **Acknowledge enthusiasm indicators**: Positive language like "I love number 2" constitutes clear approval
- **Note any personal sign-offs**: Messages ending with "xx" or friendly closures indicate strong client rapport

### 4. Next Steps After Approval

Once design is confirmed:

1. Update project status in Cairn to "Design Approved"
2. Move the approved design (Design 2 in this case) to the production-ready folder
3. Archive rejected design options with the project files
4. Schedule production timeline
5. Send production confirmation to client with estimated completion date
6. Respect holiday periods mentioned - don't send non-urgent communications during client's stated time off

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Multiple design approval**: If a client says they "love" multiple options without specifying which to proceed with, always request clarification before production.

⚠️ **Informal approval validity**: Emails from personal accounts (Yahoo, Gmail, etc.) are still valid approvals, but ensure they come from the authorized contact person.

⚠️ **Holiday timing**: If a client mentions going on holiday immediately after approval, confirm whether they expect to review proofs before production or trust you to proceed.

⚠️ **Mobile email responses**: Responses sent from mobile devices ("Sent from iPhone/Yahoo Mail") are legitimate approvals but may be brief - don't mistake brevity for lack of commitment.

## Email Communication Best Practices

### Professional Elements Demonstrated

- **Clear subject lines**: "Re: Poster design" maintains thread continuity
- **Concise presentation**: "Here are some design options" is direct and clear
- **Request for feedback**: "Let me know what you think" invites response
- **Professional closure**: "Best regards" with name and contact maintains business standards

### Client Relationship Building

This exchange shows strong client rapport:
- Enthusiastic approval ("I love number 2")
- Personal well-wishes exchanged
- Informal closures (emoji and "xx") indicate trusted relationship
- Mutual respect for personal time (holiday acknowledgment)

## File Management

After receiving approval:

1. **Rename approved file**: Change from "poster_design_option2.pdf" to "poster_design_APPROVED_[date].pdf"
2. **Update project folder**: Move to "03_Approved_Designs" subfolder
3. **Tag in Cairn**: Add "design approved" tag and client name
4. **Archive alternatives**: Move rejected options to "Archive_Design_Options" with date stamp
5. **Link approval email**: Save or link the approval email in project documentation

## Production Scheduling Considerations

- **Holiday periods**: If client is on holiday, schedule production allowing time for delivery after their return
- **Rush vs. standard**: This approval doesn't indicate urgency, so standard production timeline applies
- **Communication blackout**: Don't send non-critical updates during client's stated holiday period
