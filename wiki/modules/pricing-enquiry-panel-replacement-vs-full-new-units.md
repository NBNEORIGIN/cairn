# Pricing Enquiry: Panel Replacement vs Full New Units

## Summary

When quoting for wayfinding or display signage projects, clients may send specifications that mix **panel replacements** (into existing structures) with **full new units** (complete with posts/frames). These have significantly different material, installation, and pricing requirements. Always clarify the scope before pricing to avoid under-quoting or project delays. This article covers the key clarification questions and scope definition process using a Berwick-upon-Tweed Town Council enquiry as a case study.

## Background

**Client:** Berwick-upon-Tweed Town Council  
**Contact:** Stephen Robinson (Operations Manager)  
**Project Type:** Tourist direction maps (customer supplying artwork)  
**Initial Issue:** Purchase order descriptions unclear whether locations needed panel-only replacements or complete new installations

## Key Clarification Questions

When specifications are ambiguous, always ask:

1. **Which locations require full new units?** (frame + posts + installation from ground up)
2. **Which locations are panel replacements only?** (existing frame/structure remains, new graphic panel fitted)

### Why This Matters

| Aspect | Panel Replacement | Full New Unit |
|--------|------------------|---------------|
| **Materials** | Printed panel only | Posts, frame, fixings, concrete, panel |
| **Labour** | 1-2 hours | 4-8+ hours (groundworks, installation) |
| **Groundworks** | None | Excavation, concrete base required |
| **Planning** | Usually exempt | May require permissions |
| **Price Difference** | Baseline | 3-5x panel replacement cost |

**Misquoting this can mean absorbing thousands in unbudgeted costs.**

## Resolution Process

### Step 1: Send Clarification Email

Template used:

```
Before I proceed with pricing, I just wanted to clarify the scope 
to ensure I'm quoting accurately.

From the PO descriptions, the project may include a mix of:
- Replacement panels within existing display structures, and
- New display units complete with posts

Please confirm:
1. Which locations require full new units (frame + posts), and
2. Which are panel replacements into existing structures?
```

### Step 2: Document Client Response

**Client Response (Berwick example):**
> "Only C - Parade Car Park is attaching to an existing structure."

**Translation:**
- **Location C (Parade Car Park):** Panel replacement only
- **All other locations:** Full new units required

### Step 3: Request Site Photos

Even after clarification, request images of:
- Existing structures (for panel replacements) to verify condition and mounting method
- Proposed locations (for new units) to assess ground conditions, obstructions, access

⚠️ **Client sent image in this case** — always save to job folder and Cairn record.

## Common Pitfalls

### ❌ Assuming All Are Panel Replacements
"Tourist direction maps" suggests existing infrastructure, but this project was 90% new units. **Always verify.**

### ❌ Not Clarifying Before Site Visit
Sending an installer to measure for panels when full groundworks are needed wastes time and money.

### ❌ Quoting "Per Panel" Without Context
If client approves a "per panel" price thinking it includes installation with posts, you've created a commercial dispute.

### ❌ Ignoring Existing Structure Condition
Panel replacement assumes serviceable frame. If posts are corroded or damaged, you may need to re-scope mid-project.

## Pricing Structure

Once scope is clear, quote as separate line items:

**Option A: Itemised**
- Panel replacements @ £X per location
- Full new units @ £Y per location
- Installation (travel to Berwick-upon-Tweed as day rate)
- Groundworks/concrete (if not subcontracted)

**Option B: Per Location**
- Location A (New Unit): £X
- Location B (New Unit): £Y
- Location C (Panel Replacement): £Z

**Recommended:** Option B is clearer for client approval and matches PO line items.

## Installation Considerations for New Units

For locations requiring full new units:

1. **Statutory clearances** — verify footpath widths, visibility splays (highways)
2. **Utilities** — check for underground services before excavation
3. **Permissions** — council-owned land may still require highways consent
4. **Access** — Berwick town centre has restricted vehicle access; plan logistics
5. **Ground conditions** — coastal/historic towns may have rock, archaeology, or poor drainage
