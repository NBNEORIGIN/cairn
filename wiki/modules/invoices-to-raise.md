# Invoices to Raise

This article covers the invoice raising process for jobs where a deposit has been paid and the balance is due. It uses examples from Maid Just Right and Chrissie Howard to demonstrate the standard 25% deposit / 75% balance payment structure and requesting confirmation before proceeding with final invoicing.

## Overview

When a job reaches completion or agreed milestone, you'll need to raise the final invoice for the remaining balance. NBNE typically operates on a deposit + balance payment structure, commonly 25% upfront and 75% on completion. Before raising final invoices, always confirm the amounts with the relevant stakeholder and establish payment terms.

## Standard Payment Structure

### Deposit and Balance Split

- **Deposit**: 25% of total job value (plus VAT)
- **Balance**: 75% of total job value (plus VAT)
- **Total**: Always quoted as net amount plus VAT

### Example Calculation

**Maid Just Right Job:**
- Total job value: £535.00 (net)
- Deposit (25%): £133.75 + VAT
- Balance to invoice (75%): £401.25 + VAT

**Chrissie Howard Job:**
- Total job value: £595.00 (net)
- Deposit (25%): £148.75 + VAT
- Balance to invoice (75%): £446.25 + VAT

## Process for Raising Final Invoices

### 1. Calculate and Verify Amounts

- Confirm the original total quoted for the job
- Calculate 25% deposit and 75% balance
- Verify deposit has been received and marked as paid in Cairn
- Double-check calculations before proceeding

### 2. Request Confirmation

Before raising the balance invoice, send a confirmation request including:

- Customer name
- Amount already paid as deposit (with VAT status)
- Amount to be invoiced as balance (with VAT status)
- Total job value for transparency
- Proposed payment terms (e.g., 7 day, 14 day, 30 day)

### 3. Establish Payment Terms

Common payment terms at NBNE:
- **7 day terms**: For quick turnaround or regular customers
- **14 day terms**: Standard for most jobs
- **30 day terms**: For established account customers

Always confirm which terms to apply before raising the invoice.

### 4. Raise the Invoice in Cairn

Once confirmation received:
- Create new invoice in Cairn
- Reference the original job/quote number
- Mark as "Balance Payment" or "Final Payment"
- Apply agreed payment terms
- Ensure deposit invoice is linked or referenced
- Send to customer

## Warnings and Common Pitfalls

> **WARNING**: Always verify the deposit has actually been received and cleared before raising the balance invoice. Check bank reconciliation in Cairn.

> **COMMON MISTAKE**: Invoicing the full amount instead of just the 75% balance. This results in double-charging the customer and creates reconciliation issues.

> **VAT REMINDER**: All amounts should be quoted as net + VAT. Don't accidentally include VAT in your base calculations or you'll undercharge.

> **PAYMENT TERMS**: Never assume payment terms. Different customers have different arrangements. Always confirm before raising the invoice.

## Documentation Best Practice

When requesting confirmation for final invoices:

1. **Be clear and structured**: List each customer separately with all amounts
2. **Show your working**: Include deposit, balance, and total for transparency
3. **State VAT explicitly**: Always specify "+ VAT" to avoid confusion
4. **Request specific confirmation**: Ask about amounts AND payment terms
5. **Keep records**: File the confirmation email with the job documentation

## Customer Communication Tips

- Send balance invoices promptly when work is completed
- Include reference to original quote/order number
- Attach or reference the deposit invoice for customer records
- Make it easy for customers to reconcile their payments
- Follow up on overdue balances according to the payment terms agreed
