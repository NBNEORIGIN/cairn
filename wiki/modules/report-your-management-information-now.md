# Report Your Management Information Now

This article covers the monthly requirement to report Management Information (MI) to the Crown Commercial Service (CCS, formerly GCA) for NBNE's commercial agreements. MI reporting is a contractual obligation that must be completed by the 9th of each month, regardless of whether any business was conducted under the agreement. Failure to report on time results in overdue reminders and potential contract compliance issues.

## What is Management Information Reporting?

Management Information is data about sales made through government framework agreements. CCS uses this data to:

- Manage and improve commercial agreements
- Collect management charges based on sales
- Shape future commercial agreements and procurement strategies

For NBNE, this primarily relates to **RM6237 – Low Value Purchase System** framework agreement.

## Reporting Deadline

**Monthly deadline: 9th of the following month**

For example, March 2026 business must be reported by 9 April 2026.

## How to Report MI

1. **Access the reporting portal**
   - Go to: https://www.reportmi.gca.gov.uk
   - Log in using your credentials (contact IT if you've forgotten login details)

2. **Select the correct agreement**
   - Choose RM6237 – Low Value Purchase System from your active agreements
   - Select the reporting period (e.g., March 2026)

3. **Enter sales data**
   - Input total sales value for the month
   - Include all relevant transaction details as required by the form
   - Ensure VAT figures are correctly calculated

4. **Report nil returns if applicable**
   - If no business was conducted, you must still log in and submit a nil return
   - Do not skip months with no activity

5. **Submit and confirm**
   - Review all entered data before submission
   - Save confirmation reference for your records
   - You should receive an email confirmation

## ⚠️ Warnings and Common Pitfalls

**Critical reminders:**

- **NIL RETURNS MUST STILL BE SUBMITTED** – This is the most common mistake. Even months with zero sales require a submission.
- **Late reporting is a breach of contract** – This can affect NBNE's standing on the framework and future tender applications.
- **Don't wait for reminders** – Set a monthly calendar reminder for the 5th-8th of each month to complete MI reporting before the deadline.
- **One person responsibility** – Currently this falls to Toby Fletcher, but ensure holiday cover is arranged with a deputy who has portal access.

**Data accuracy issues:**

- Ensure you're reporting the correct month's data
- Double-check that all framework sales are included (check Cairn and accounts system)
- Don't include non-framework sales in the figures
- Keep source documentation in case of audits

## Setting Up Reminders

To avoid overdue situations:

1. **Add to Outlook calendar**: Create a recurring monthly task for the 6th of each month titled "MI Reporting Due by 9th"
2. **Set deputy access**: Ensure at least one other person can access the portal during holidays
3. **Keep a checklist**: Note which months have been completed in the shared Operations folder

## Troubleshooting

**If you receive an overdue reminder:**
- Log in immediately and complete the outstanding report
- Check if multiple months are outstanding
- Reply to the GCA team acknowledging completion

**If you can't access the portal:**
- Contact: report-mi@gca.gov.uk
- Include your company name, framework reference (RM6237), and describe the issue
- Allow 2-3 working days for response

**If you're unsure about figures:**
- Check with Finance team for framework sales data
- Review Cairn for relevant customer orders
- It's better to submit an estimate on time and correct later than miss the deadline
