# Tell Me Your Target Audience

This is a cold outreach email from Selectabase Ltd, a data broker offering to sell mailing lists and contact databases. The sender is attempting to initiate a sales conversation by asking NBNE to define its ideal target audience, promising to return with database pricing and reach statistics. This type of solicitation is common for B2B companies and should be handled according to NBNE's procurement and marketing policies.

## Email Summary

**Sender:** James Flashman, Managing Director at Selectabase Ltd  
**Company:** Selectabase Ltd (Dover-based data/mailing list broker)  
**Purpose:** Sales prospecting for B2B contact databases and mailing lists  
**Key Offer:** Targeted mailing lists for the Alnwick area, TPS-clean B2B databases

## What They're Selling

Selectabase provides:

1. **Targeted mailing lists** - Physical mailing addresses for direct mail campaigns
2. **B2B contact databases** - Business contact information (emails, phone numbers, addresses)
3. **TPS-clean lists** - Data scrubbed against the Telephone Preference Service register
4. **Geographic targeting** - Specific mention of Alnwick/local area targeting

## Should You Respond?

**Consider only if:**

- NBNE has an approved budget for purchasing marketing data
- You have explicit approval from management to explore direct mail campaigns
- NBNE's marketing strategy includes outbound prospecting via purchased lists
- You are authorised to make procurement decisions in this category

**Do NOT respond if:**

- You are unsure of NBNE's current marketing strategy
- No budget has been allocated for purchased data
- You have not been asked to explore this option by leadership

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Warning:** Purchasing contact databases can create GDPR compliance risks. Any purchased data must come with legitimate interest or consent documentation.

⚠️ **Cost Escalation:** Initial quotes are often low to secure engagement. Total campaign costs (data + printing + postage) add up quickly.

⚠️ **Data Quality Issues:** Purchased lists frequently contain outdated information, wrong contacts, or poor targeting. ROI is often disappointing.

⚠️ **Unsolicited Follow-up:** Responding typically triggers persistent follow-up from sales representatives.

## If You Choose to Engage

Should management approve exploration of this option, ensure you:

1. **Request data provenance documentation** - How was the data collected? Is consent documented?
2. **Ask for sample data** - Review quality before purchasing
3. **Clarify GDPR compliance** - Request their Data Protection Officer contact and processing agreements
4. **Get pricing in writing** - Including all costs (data, refreshes, licensing terms)
5. **Understand usage restrictions** - Single use vs. multi-use licensing
6. **Check references** - Request case studies from similar print/sign businesses

## Recommended Response Options

### Option 1: Not Interested (Recommended for most cases)
Simply delete or archive the email. No response required for unsolicited sales emails.

### Option 2: Polite Decline
If persistent follow-up occurs, send a brief response:
> "Thank you for reaching out. NBNE is not currently pursuing purchased data solutions. Please remove us from your contact list."

### Option 3: Forward to Decision Maker
If this aligns with current initiatives, forward to the appropriate manager or marketing lead with context.
