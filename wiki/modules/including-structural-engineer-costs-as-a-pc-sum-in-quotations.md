# Including Structural Engineer Costs as a PC Sum in Quotations

## Summary

When a project requires independent structural engineering input (e.g., freestanding signage posts, wind loading calculations, foundation design), the customer may request this cost to be included as a Provisional or Prime Cost (PC) sum within your quotation rather than commissioning the engineer separately. This allows the total project cost to be presented in a single document while maintaining transparency about which elements are estimated third-party costs versus NBNE's direct supply and installation charges.

## When This Situation Arises

Structural engineer involvement is typically required for:

- **Freestanding post-mounted signage in public areas** (car parks, town centres, parks)
- **Large format displays requiring wind loading calculations** to BS EN 1991-1-4
- **Ground-fixed installations** where foundation design must account for soil conditions
- **Projects where NBNE cannot act as design authority** due to conflict of interest (we are both designer and installer)

The customer may prefer a single quotation covering all elements rather than managing multiple suppliers.

## How to Structure the Quotation

### 1. Confirm the Requirement in Writing

When the customer requests inclusion of structural engineering costs, acknowledge clearly:

- That you will include it as a **PC sum** (defined cost allowance for third-party specialist work)
- That this is an estimate based on typical project scope
- That final costs will be confirmed once the engineer is appointed and scope is agreed

### 2. Break Down the Quotation into Clear Sections

**Section A: NBNE Direct Supply & Installation**
- Manufacturing of display units/panels
- Posts, frames, and hardware
- Installation labour
- Site survey (if included)
- Project management

**Section B: PC Sum – Structural Engineering Services**
- Design calculations (wind loading, structural integrity)
- Foundation specification
- Installation method statement review
- Professional certification/sign-off

State clearly: *"This is a Provisional Cost Sum based on similar projects. Actual cost will be confirmed following appointment of structural engineer and scope agreement. Any variation will be subject to prior approval."*

**Section C: Total Project Cost**
- Sum of Section A + Section B

### 3. Obtain an Indicative Quote from a Structural Consultant

Before issuing your quotation:

- Contact a structural engineering firm we have worked with previously (check Cairn for approved suppliers)
- Provide outline scope: number of units, typical heights, ground conditions if known, location/wind exposure
- Request an **indicative fee estimate** for design and certification
- Use this figure (with small contingency if appropriate) as your PC sum

⚠️ **Warning:** Do not guess structural engineering costs. Always base PC sums on actual indicative quotes, even if preliminary. Under-estimating creates client dissatisfaction and potential cost disputes later.

### 4. Include Key Assumptions and Exclusions

Clearly note in the quotation:

- PC sum assumes standard scope (e.g., "up to 6 freestanding units, maximum 3m height, standard wind exposure")
- Ground investigation or soil testing (if required) may be additional
- Any design revisions requested by planning authority or client may incur additional fees
- Engineer appointment and contract will be managed by [NBNE / the client - confirm which]

### 5. Clarify Procurement Route

Specify in your covering email or quotation notes:

**Option 1:** NBNE appoints the structural engineer on the client's behalf, manages the relationship, and includes costs in our invoice (PC sum is then recharged)

**Option 2:** Client approves the PC sum amount but appoints the engineer directly and pays them separately

Confirm which approach the client prefers. Option 1 is simpler for the client but requires us to manage an additional supplier relationship.

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Underestimating complexity:** Public realm signage often requires more detailed engineering input than anticipated (especially in coastal/exposed locations like Berwick-upon-Tweed). Build in contingency.

⚠️ **Failing to update the PC sum:** If project scope changes (e.g., more units added, heights increased), revisit the structural engineering estimate immediately.

⚠️ **Not documenting scope:** Ensure the engineer's scope matches what you've quoted. Misalignment leads to gaps in responsibility.

⚠️ **Assuming client understands PC sums:** Not all clients are familiar with construction terminology. Explain clearly that this is an estimated allowance for third-party costs.

## Follow-Up Actions

- Once quotation is accepted, immediately engage the structural engineer to confirm scope and fee
- Provide engineer with all relevant site information, product specifications, and installation methods
- Ensure engineer's outputs (calculations, foundation drawings) are received before manufacturing commences
- Confirm final engineer cost with client before invoicing if it varies from PC sum
