# Handling Personal Projects and GCSE Coursework Requests

## Summary

Occasionally, NBNE receives enquiries for personal projects, including student coursework such as GCSE Design & Technology projects. While we aim to be helpful to the community, these jobs often present scheduling challenges, tight margins, and potential complications that can disrupt commercial workflow. This article outlines how to handle such requests professionally while protecting business operations.

## Types of Personal Project Enquiries

### 1. GCSE/A-Level Coursework
Students typically need:
- Small-scale cutting/fabrication (often single pieces)
- Quick turnaround (driven by school deadlines)
- Materials advice (they may not know specifications)
- Budget constraints (personal/family funding)

### 2. Hobby and Maker Projects
- One-off prototypes
- Personal gifts or home items
- Small business trials

## Handling the Initial Enquiry

1. **Assess the scope immediately** - Check file formats, material requirements, and timeline expectations in the first conversation
2. **Be transparent about scheduling** - Commercial work takes priority; communicate realistic lead times
3. **Offer an in-person consultation** - This helps gauge project viability and sets expectations (as in the Kit's Project example where a 10:30 meeting was arranged)
4. **Request files in advance** - Ask for DXF, AI, or other working files via email before any meeting to check compatibility and spot potential issues

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Short Notice Cancellations** - As seen in the example, personal projects often get cancelled after initial consultation due to timeline changes, design revisions, or budget realization. Don't block out machine time until deposit is received.

⚠️ **Underestimating Setup Time** - A single piece of 3mm acrylic takes nearly as much setup as a commercial run. Price accordingly.

⚠️ **File Quality Issues** - Student-generated DXF files may have broken vectors, incorrect scaling, or unsuitable geometries. Always review files before quoting.

⚠️ **Material Misconceptions** - Customers may request inappropriate materials for their application. Be prepared to educate and suggest alternatives.

⚠️ **Timeline Incompatibility** - Personal projects rarely align with production schedules. Making special accommodations disrupts workflow.

## Recommended Approach

### Option 1: Polite Redirection
For small or complex personal projects:
- Recommend local makerspaces or FabLabs with public access laser cutters
- Suggest school workshop facilities for coursework
- Provide contact details for operators who specialize in one-offs

### Option 2: Minimum Charge Policy
If accepting the work:
- Implement a minimum job charge (e.g., £50-75) regardless of material cost
- Require 50% deposit for all personal projects
- Schedule only during identified slack periods
- Make clear that commercial work may push back personal project timelines

### Option 3: Batching System
- Designate specific days (e.g., last Friday of month) for small personal jobs
- Collect multiple small projects to run together
- Communicate these fixed dates to enquirers upfront

## Professional Communication Template

When a project won't work out (as in Vicky's follow-up):

- Acknowledge the cancellation professionally
- Don't express frustration even if you've invested consultation time
- Leave the door open for future commercial opportunities
- Keep records brief but complete for CRM purposes

**Example Response:**
> "Hi Vicky, no problem at all - thanks for letting me know. Best of luck with Kit's project. If you need any signage work in future, please get in touch."

## Business Considerations

**Pros of accepting personal projects:**
- Community goodwill and local reputation
- Potential future commercial customers (parents, teachers, schools)
- Skills development on unusual requests
- Material offcut utilization

**Cons:**
- Administrative overhead disproportionate to revenue
- Schedule disruption
- Higher cancellation rates
- Price sensitivity and negotiation

## Record Keeping

Even for declined jobs:
- Log enquiry in CRM with "Personal Project - Declined" tag
- Save any files provided (may indicate commercial opportunity later)
- Note if enquirer has business connections worth maintaining
- Track enquiry sources (referrals, website, walk-ins)
