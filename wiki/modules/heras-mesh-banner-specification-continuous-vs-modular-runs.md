# Heras Mesh Banner Specification – Continuous vs. Modular Runs

## Summary

When quoting large-format Heras fence branding (particularly runs over 10m), always present both **continuous** and **modular** mesh banner options to the client. This avoids specification disputes post-PO, accounts for handling and fixing realities on construction sites, and gives the client choice based on their site conditions. This approach is particularly important when re-costing from initial budgetary figures to formal quotations.

---

## When to Offer Both Options

- Mesh banners **over 10 linear metres** in a single run
- Projects where the client has approved a budgetary figure but not yet seen a detailed specification
- Construction sites where handling, storage, or fixing logistics may be constrained
- Any job where the original pricing was based on assumptions rather than confirmed client preference

---

## How to Structure the Options

### Option 1 – Continuous Run

**Description:**  
One seamless mesh banner spanning the full linear measurement (e.g. 1.75m × 25m), hemmed and eyeletted along all edges.

**Advantages:**  
- Cleaner, uninterrupted visual appearance
- No visible joins or seams
- Fewer individual components to track

**Disadvantages:**  
- Heavy and awkward to transport
- Requires multiple operatives to fix safely on Heras panels
- Difficult to replace if damaged – entire banner must be removed
- Higher risk of wind damage due to surface area
- May require specialist transport or rolling

**Typical use case:**  
High-profile frontages where aesthetics outweigh handling practicality.

---

### Option 2 – Modular Run (Recommended)

**Description:**  
Multiple individual mesh panels (typically 4m × 1.75m each), hemmed and eyeletted, which tile together to form the full run (e.g. six panels totalling ~24–25m).

**Advantages:**  
- Easier to transport in a standard van
- Can be fixed by 1–2 operatives without specialist lifting
- Individual panels can be replaced if torn or damaged
- Easier to adjust or reposition on site
- Lower wind load per panel

**Disadvantages:**  
- Visible seams every ~4m (though typically unnoticeable at street distance)
- Slightly higher material waste due to multiple hems
- More components to label, track, and install

**Typical use case:**  
Construction site perimeter branding, temporary hoarding, long Heras runs.

---

## Pricing Considerations

⚠️ **Warning:** Continuous runs over 15m often involve additional material handling, reinforced eyeletting, and increased labour during finishing. Do not assume linear scaling from smaller banners.

**Factors that affect price:**

- **Material yield** – a 25m banner may require a full roll with higher waste
- **Finishing time** – hemming and eyeletting a single 25m banner is more labour-intensive than six 4m panels
- **QC and handling** – continuous banners require larger tables and more careful inspection
- **Transport** – modular formats stack flat; continuous banners must be rolled and may incur courier surcharges

**General guidance:**  
Price modular runs at **£7–9 per linear metre** (depending on height). Continuous runs over 15m should be quoted at **£9–12 per linear metre** to account for handling premium.

---

## What to Include in the Email

When presenting both options to a client, include:

1. **Clear labelling** – "Option 1" and "Option 2" with a recommendation flag
2. **Practical explanation** – describe the trade-offs in terms relevant to their site (e.g. ease of fixing, replacement, wind resistance)
3. **Honest context** – if re-costing from an earlier budget figure, explain why the specification has been revised (see example in source email)
4. **Line-item pricing** – show the cost for each option separately so the client can make an informed choice

---

## Information Required to Progress

Once the client selects their preferred option, confirm the following before raising the order:

- Preferred mesh format (Option 1 or Option 2)
- Substrate material for any rigid panels (e.g. ACM, Dibond, Correx)
- Logo files in vector format (AI, EPS, or PDF preferred)
- Delivery contact name and phone number for site
- Any specific fixing requirements (e.g. cable ties provided, pre-drilled eyelets)

---

## Lead Time and Deposit

- Standard lead time: **5–7 working days** from artwork approval
- Deposit: **25%** to commence artwork and production
- Artwork proofs should be issued within 24–48 hours of receiving vector files

---

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Under-specifying at budgetary stage:** Always note on initial estimates if large-format mesh pricing is provisional and subject to specification confirmation.

⚠️ **Assuming client preference:** Don't default to continuous runs just because the client said "25m banner" – they may not understand the practical implications.

⚠️ **Ignoring site logistics:** Construction sites often have narrow access, limited storage, and lone operatives. Modular is almost always more practical.
