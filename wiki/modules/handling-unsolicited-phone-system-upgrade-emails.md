# Handling Unsolicited Phone System Upgrade Emails

## Summary

This is a common spam/marketing email that claims NBNE's phone systems are "due for an upgrade." These emails use scare tactics and false urgency to generate leads for telecoms sales companies. They are NOT legitimate system notifications and should be deleted immediately. The sender has no actual knowledge of NBNE's infrastructure or telecommunications needs.

## Identification Characteristics

These fraudulent upgrade solicitations typically display the following features:

1. **Personalized subject line** - Uses recipient's name to appear legitimate ("Toby Fletcher, do you have the best Telephone System...")
2. **False urgency** - Claims systems are "due" for upgrade without any actual assessment
3. **Generic messaging** - Vague statements like "outdated phone systems getting in the way of business"
4. **Tracking links** - Long, obfuscated URLs containing tracking parameters (expertmarket.com domains are common)
5. **Business type selection** - Offers to "compare quotes" based on business category
6. **No sender authentication** - Does not come from known NBNE suppliers or IT department

## Proper Handling Steps

1. **Do NOT click any links** - All links contain tracking pixels and will confirm your email address is active, leading to more spam
2. **Delete immediately** - Move to trash without engaging
3. **Report as spam** - Use your email client's spam reporting function to help filter future messages
4. **Do NOT unsubscribe** - Unsubscribe links in unsolicited emails often verify your address is monitored
5. **Alert IT Security** - If these emails bypass spam filters regularly, forward one example to IT security for filter refinement

## ⚠️ Warning: Common Pitfalls

**Never provide information in response to these emails, even if:**
- They claim to know your current vendor contract end date
- They reference specific equipment models or technologies
- They offer "free assessments" or "no-obligation quotes"
- They create false urgency about security vulnerabilities or compliance

**These tactics are designed to:**
- Harvest business intelligence about your organization
- Generate commission-based sales leads
- Potentially facilitate social engineering attacks
- Build profiles for targeted phishing campaigns

## How Legitimate Upgrades Actually Work at NBNE

For operators unfamiliar with proper procurement channels, here's how real telecommunications upgrades are initiated:

1. **IT Department Assessment** - Infrastructure reviews are conducted internally on planned schedules
2. **Formal RFP Process** - When upgrades are needed, procurement issues formal Requests for Proposal
3. **Known Vendor Relationships** - NBNE works with established, vetted telecommunications partners
4. **Internal Communication** - Upgrade notifications come through official NBNE channels (Cairn, internal email, team meetings)
5. **Budget Allocation** - All major system changes go through financial planning cycles

**If you're unsure whether your department needs phone system upgrades, contact IT Service Desk through proper channels.**

## Additional Context

The company "Expert Market" mentioned in these emails operates as a lead generation service. They collect information from respondents and sell it to multiple competing vendors, who then aggressively contact you. This is a business model, not a scam per se, but engaging with these emails:

- Wastes operational time fielding sales calls
- May expose sensitive business information
- Creates vendor relationship confusion
- Bypasses NBNE's procurement controls and vendor vetting processes

## Red Flags Checklist

Mark as spam if the email includes:
- [ ] Unsolicited claims about your infrastructure status
- [ ] Time-pressure language ("due for upgrade," "don't miss out")
- [ ] Generic "business phone system" messaging
- [ ] Request to select your business type
- [ ] Quote comparison services
- [ ] Long tracking URLs
- [ ] No prior vendor relationship
- [ ] Sender domain not matching any known supplier
