## Quote Preparation Steps

1. **Review customer-supplied specifications** - Check attached details for sizes and locations
2. **Clarify installation scope** - Confirm which locations need full units vs. replacements (DONE in this case)
3. **Await site imagery** - Customer has provided image of Location C area
4. **Calculate materials:**
   - Location C: Panel only + fixings for existing structure
   - Other locations: Full units (frame, panel, posts, concrete bases)
5. **Price installation accordingly:**
   - Location C: Lower installation cost (existing structure)
   - Other locations: Full installation including groundworks
6. **Factor in Berwick-upon-Tweed travel** - Northumberland location, check distance from workshop

## Important Warnings

⚠️ **CRITICAL: Always clarify new vs. replacement before quoting.** Installation costs for attaching to existing structures are significantly lower than full new installations with posts and groundworks. Misquoting this can lead to substantial margin loss.

⚠️ **Customer-supplied artwork:** Ensure quote clearly states artwork supply is customer responsibility. Build in review time to check artwork files are print-ready before committing to lead times.

⚠️ **Site survey considerations:** For Location C (existing structure attachment), verify structure condition and suitability. May require site visit or additional photos to confirm fixing method.

⚠️ **Council procurement:** Local authority customer - may require specific quote format, purchase order process, or payment terms. Confirm requirements upfront.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Assuming all locations are the same type** - Always ask for location-by-location breakdown when multiple sites mentioned
- **Not requesting site photos** - Existing structures may not be suitable for attachment; get visual confirmation early
- **Underestimating council installation requirements** - May need specific health & safety documentation, permits, or parking arrangements
- **Missing artwork specification requirements** - Clarify file formats, resolution requirements, and approval process upfront
