# Handling Low-Resolution Logos for Large Format Printing

When a customer's logo file is low resolution (raster/bitmap format), it will appear blurred or pixelated when enlarged for signage. This article covers how to identify logo quality issues early, present solutions to customers, and manage the vectorisation process to maintain print quality while controlling costs and timelines.

## Identifying Logo Quality Issues

1. **Check logo files immediately upon job handover**
   - Open the file in Adobe Illustrator or similar software
   - Zoom to 200-400% to check for pixelation or blur
   - Check file format: JPG, PNG, and BMP are raster formats; SVG, AI, EPS, and PDF (vector-based) are preferred
   - Assess whether resolution is adequate for the intended print size

2. **Calculate if resolution is sufficient**
   - For outdoor signage, minimum 150 DPI at final print size is acceptable
   - For close-viewing applications, aim for 300 DPI
   - If the logo is 500px wide and needs to print at 2000mm, the quality will be inadequate

3. **Document the issue with visual evidence**
   - Take a screenshot showing the blur/pixelation (as done with "Glendale logo close up.PNG")
   - This helps customers understand the problem visually
   - Attach to your email when contacting the customer

## Presenting Options to the Customer

When contacting the customer about logo quality issues, always provide **three clear options** in order of quality:

1. **Request a vector file (SVG, AI, EPS, or high-res PDF)**
   - Most cost-effective solution if the customer has the file
   - Ask specifically: "Do you have the logo in SVG format or as a vector file from your designer?"
   - No additional cost, minimal delay (usually 24-48 hours for customer to locate)

2. **Full vectorisation service**
   - We manually redraw the logo to create a perfect vector version
   - Guarantees crisp, high-quality finish at any size
   - Be upfront: this takes time and incurs additional cost
   - Typical turnaround: 2-3 working days depending on logo complexity
   - Quote this option but don't quote specific pricing in initial email (check current rates in Cairn first)

3. **Simplified vectorisation**
   - Create a cleaner, simplified version of the logo
   - More cost-effective than full vectorisation
   - Useful for complex logos where exact recreation isn't critical
   - Set expectations: final version may differ slightly from original

4. **Proceed with current file**
   - Always offer this as the final option
   - Include clear warning: "may not reproduce at the best quality when printed at scale"
   - Protects NBNE from quality complaints later
   - Some customers prefer to proceed if it's for temporary/budget signage

## Email Communication Best Practices

- **Subject line**: Keep the original subject thread when following up on a job handover
- **Tone**: Professional but approachable; avoid technical jargon like "raster" or "DPI" unless the customer demonstrates understanding
- **Timing**: Flag logo issues within 24 hours of job handover to avoid delays
- **Handover context**: Mention who you're taking over from (e.g., "taking over from Toby") to provide continuity
- **Action required**: End with clear next steps—"Please let me know how you'd like to proceed"

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Never proceed with printing a low-resolution logo without written customer approval** – this can lead to complaints and reprint costs.

⚠️ **Don't assume the customer doesn't have a vector file** – always ask first, even if they've only sent a JPG. Many customers don't understand file formats but may have the correct version stored.

⚠️ **Avoid quoting vectorisation costs in initial enquiry** – pricing can vary based on complexity. Check current rates and get approval from production team first.

⚠️ **Don't over-promise on timelines** – vectorisation takes time. Build in buffer for revisions and customer approval.

⚠️ **Watch for scope creep** – if the customer requests colour changes or design modifications during vectorisation, this is additional work and should be quoted separately.

## Recording the Decision in Cairn

Once the customer responds:

1. Log their chosen option in the job notes
2. If proceeding with low-res file, attach their written approval to the job
3. Update timeline if vectorisation is required
4. If vectorisation is approved, assign to the design team with all source files
5. Flag the job for quality check before final production
