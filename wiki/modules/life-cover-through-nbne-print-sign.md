# Life Cover Through NBNE Print & Sign

## Summary

Life insurance for business owners and key employees can be structured as a Relevant Life Cover policy, allowing the company to pay premiums as an allowable business expense rather than the individual paying from post-tax income. This arrangement provides significant tax advantages: no P11D benefit-in-kind reporting, no National Insurance contributions, and no income tax liability for the employee. This article explains how to handle these enquiries within the NBNE framework.

## What is Relevant Life Cover?

Relevant Life Cover is a life insurance policy designed specifically for employees (including directors) paid for by their employer. Unlike traditional life insurance paid personally or group life schemes, it offers:

- **Tax-deductible expense** for the company (reduces Corporation Tax)
- **No P11D reporting** required
- **No National Insurance** for employer or employee
- **No income tax** for the employee on the benefit
- **Pays out outside the estate** for Inheritance Tax purposes

This makes it particularly attractive for company directors and business owners who currently pay for life insurance from post-tax personal income.

## Handling Relevant Life Cover Enquiries

### 1. Initial Client Contact

When a client enquires about life cover:

- Confirm they are working with an FCA-regulated adviser (check FCA number)
- Explain NBNE's role is document preparation only
- If they don't have an adviser, provide general signposting to FCA register (fca.org.uk/register)
- Do not discuss policy specifics, tax advantages, or suitability

### 2. Working with Regulated Advisers

The enquiry in this example comes from **1st Call 4 Mortgages** (FCA: 676588), a regulated firm. When working with such advisers:

- Request a clear brief of what documents are required
- Confirm what information needs to be included in client-facing materials
- Ensure all technical content comes from the regulated adviser
- Position documents as "for review and signature" not as advice

### 3. Document Preparation

Typical documents may include:

- Board minutes approving the policy as a company expense
- Director/employee consent forms
- Instructions to insurance provider
- Company resolution confirming commercial justification

**Process:**
1. Receive document brief from regulated adviser
2. Prepare draft using Print & Sign templates
3. Include standard disclaimers confirming advice was provided by FCA-regulated firm
4. Send to adviser for technical review
5. Issue final version for client signature

### 4. Key Information to Gather

From the regulated adviser:
- Policy provider and reference
- Covered employee details
- Premium amount and frequency
- Commercial justification (e.g., key person, remuneration package)
- Confirmation of advice given

From the client company:
- Company details and registration number
- Board approval date
- Signatories for resolutions

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Regulatory boundary issues**: Never draft the substantive content about tax benefits or policy suitability. This must come from the regulated adviser.

⚠️ **Scope creep**: Clients may ask "Is this worth doing?" Always redirect to their regulated adviser.

⚠️ **HMRC compliance**: Ensure commercial justification is documented. HMRC may challenge deductions without proper reasoning.

⚠️ **Mixing services**: Keep Relevant Life Cover documentation separate from general company secretarial work - different regulatory requirements apply.
