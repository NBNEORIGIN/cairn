# Fresh Print Ideas for Print Business Expansion

This marketing email from Roland DG Europe highlights four print technology solutions aimed at helping print businesses expand their service offerings and improve operational efficiency. The email showcases direct-to-object UV printing, integrated print-and-cut systems, direct-to-film (DTF) technology, and design workflow software. While presented as industry developments, this is promotional content for Roland DG's specific product lineup.

## Featured Technologies

### 1. Direct-to-Object UV Printing (MO Series)

**Capability**: Print directly onto three-dimensional objects up to 204mm in diameter

**Compatible Materials**:
- Plastic and acrylic
- Wood and metal
- Glass
- Cardboard and leather

**Business Application**: This technology enables print shops to move beyond flat substrates and offer promotional product customization, personalized merchandise, and specialty item printing without outsourcing.

### 2. Integrated Print and Cut (TrueVIS XG-640)

**Key Specifications**:
- Combined print and cut workflow in single device
- Production speed: up to 15.2 m²/h
- Eliminates transfer between separate printing and cutting equipment

**Efficiency Gains**: Single-device workflow reduces handling time, potential registration errors, and floor space requirements compared to separate print and cut stations.

### 3. Direct-to-Film Technology (TY-300i)

**Award Recognition**: 2026 BLI Pick Award recipient

**Performance Highlights**:
- Durability through 20+ wash-and-dry cycles
- Strong speed and print quality ratings from independent testing
- Suitable for apparel decoration and textile applications

**DTF Context**: Direct-to-film printing transfers designs from special film to fabric, offering an alternative to direct-to-garment (DTG) or screen printing for textile decoration.

### 4. Design Workflow Software (Roland DG Connect Designer)

**Purpose**: File preparation and production workflow optimization

**Claimed Benefits**:
- Reduced file preparation time
- Fewer errors during production handoff
- Faster job turnaround
- Decreased dependence on external design resources

**Workflow Position**: This software sits between customer file intake and production equipment, acting as a bridge to standardize and optimize files for Roland DG printing systems.

## Evaluation Considerations

### ⚠️ Important Context

**This is vendor marketing material**. Before making purchasing decisions based on this email:

- **Verify specifications independently** - Marketing claims should be validated through third-party reviews, direct testing, or reference customers
- **Calculate actual ROI** - Production speed and efficiency claims must be tested against your specific job mix and workflow
- **Consider vendor lock-in** - The Connect Designer software appears proprietary to Roland DG equipment
- **Compare alternatives** - Other manufacturers offer competing solutions in each category
- **Assess training requirements** - New technology categories require staff training and workflow adjustment

### Due Diligence Checklist

If considering any of these technologies:

1. **Request demonstration** with your actual files and substrates
2. **Speak with existing users** in similar market segments
3. **Review total cost of ownership** including consumables, maintenance, and service contracts
4. **Evaluate workflow integration** with existing systems and processes
5. **Verify claimed durability** through accelerated testing or extended sample trials
6. **Check service and support availability** in your geographic area
