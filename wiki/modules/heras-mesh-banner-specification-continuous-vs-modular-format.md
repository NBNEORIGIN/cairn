# Heras Mesh Banner Specification – Continuous vs Modular Format

## Summary

When quoting large-format Heras fence branding (particularly banners over 15m in length), always present both continuous and modular mesh banner options to the client. Continuous runs offer a cleaner visual but are harder to handle and fix on site. Modular panels (typically 4m widths) are easier to transport, install, and replace if damaged. This approach manages client expectations, reduces installation complaints, and gives the client control over the format that suits their site logistics best. Always correct under-specifications **before** PO stage to avoid disputes later.

## When to Offer Both Options

- Banner runs **over 15 linear metres**
- Sites with limited labour or access constraints
- Projects where panels may need individual replacement (high-traffic or exposed locations)
- Clients unfamiliar with the practical differences between formats

## Option Breakdown

### Option 1: Continuous Run

**Description:**  
One seamless mesh banner spanning the full length (e.g. 1.75m × 25m), hemmed and eyeletted as a single piece.

**Advantages:**  
- Cleaner, uninterrupted visual appearance
- Fewer seams or joins visible

**Disadvantages:**  
- Heavy and awkward to handle on site (25m+ length)
- Requires multiple operatives to fix safely
- Difficult to replace if one section is damaged
- Higher risk of snagging or tearing during installation

**Typical use case:**  
Prestige projects where visual continuity is prioritised and professional install teams are guaranteed.

---

### Option 2: Modular Run (Recommended)

**Description:**  
Multiple individual mesh panels (typically 4m × 1.75m each), hemmed and eyeletted separately, totalling the required linear metreage (e.g. six panels ≈ 24–25m).

**Advantages:**  
- Easier to transport in standard vehicles
- Simpler to fix by smaller site teams (one or two people per panel)
- Individual panels can be replaced if damaged without replacing entire run
- Reduced risk of handling damage

**Disadvantages:**  
- Visible seams between panels (typically minimal at standard viewing distance)
- Slight variance in total length due to panel overlap

**Typical use case:**  
Standard construction sites, repeat clients, or projects where site teams handle their own installation.

---

## Pricing and Presentation

- **Present both options in the same quotation** with line items clearly labelled "Option 1" and "Option 2"
- Indicate "(recommended)" next to the modular option unless the client has specifically requested continuous
- Ensure pricing reflects the true material, finishing, and handling cost for each format
- If the original budget estimate was based on underspecified assumptions (e.g. modular pricing quoted for a continuous banner), **flag this proactively before PO**

### Example Quotation Line Items

```
9a. Branded Heras fence mesh banner – Option 1 (continuous 1.75m × 25m) – £XXX each (x2)
9b. Branded Heras fence mesh banner – Option 2 (modular: 6 panels per run, ~24–25m total) – £XXX per run (x2) [RECOMMENDED]
```

## Information Required to Progress

Once the client confirms their preferred option, collect:

1. **Confirmation of mesh format** (Option 1 or Option 2)
2. **Material confirmation** for any rigid panels (ACM, Foamex, Dibond, etc.)
3. **Vector logo files** (AI, EPS, or high-res PDF preferred)
4. **Site delivery contact** (name and mobile number)

## Lead Time and Deposit

- Standard lead time: **5–7 working days** from artwork approval
- Raise **25% deposit invoice** on order acceptance to commence artwork and production
- Artwork proofs to be issued within 24–48 hours of receiving logos and confirmation

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Under-specifying continuous banners in initial budgets.** Always cost both formats from the outset, especially if the client's original enquiry is vague on format preference.

⚠️ **Assuming the client understands the practical difference.** Many clients (especially those new to site signage) don't realise how difficult a 25m continuous banner is to install. Spell it out.

⚠️ **Waiting until after PO to flag cost differences.** If you've under-costed, raise it at quotation stage—clients appreciate honesty before commitment.

⚠️ **Not recommending modular format.** Unless there's a compelling visual reason, modular is almost always the better choice for the client and reduces our liability for installation issues.
