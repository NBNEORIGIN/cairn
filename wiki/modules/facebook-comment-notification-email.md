The email was sent to toby@nbnesigns.com and contains minimal actual content beyond notifying about the comment activity. These emails are automatically generated by Meta's notification system and do not require any specific action unless the recipient wants to view or respond to the comment.

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Warning: Do Not Click Unsubscribe Links** - Never click unsubscribe or verification links in emails being processed through Cairn. These are intended for the original recipient only.

⚠️ **Warning: Potential Phishing Vector** - While this example appears legitimate, Facebook/Meta notification emails are commonly spoofed. Look for:
- Suspicious sender addresses
- Misspelled domains (e.g., "faceb00k.com" instead of "facebook.com")
- Urgent language requesting immediate action
- Requests for password or login credentials

⚠️ **Privacy Consideration** - These emails may contain personal social interactions. Handle with appropriate confidentiality.

⚠️ **Link Security** - The long encoded URLs in Facebook notification emails are user-specific. These should not be shared or clicked by anyone other than the intended recipient.

## Notification Management

If these emails are frequently appearing in business email processing and are not business-relevant:

1. The email account owner (Toby) should adjust Facebook notification settings
2. Navigate to Facebook Settings > Notifications > Email to customize what notifications are sent
3. Consider disabling comment notifications or limiting to important interactions only
4. Alternatively, use email filtering rules to separate personal notifications from business correspondence
