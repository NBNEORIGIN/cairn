# Scoping Enquiries: Replacement Panels vs. New Units

## Summary

When quoting for display signage projects, especially tourist information boards and directional maps, it's critical to establish whether the client requires **replacement panels only** (artwork updates in existing frames) or **complete new units** (frame, posts, and installation). This distinction fundamentally impacts pricing, installation requirements, and potentially planning permissions. This article outlines the scoping process using a Berwick-upon-Tweed Town Council enquiry as a case study.

## Why This Matters

**Pricing差异**: A replacement panel typically costs 40-60% less than a full new unit with posts and groundworks. Misunderstanding the scope can result in:
- Lost quotes due to overpricing
- Underbidding that erodes margin
- Client dissatisfaction when expectations don't match deliverables

**Installation complexity**: Panel replacements may be completed in hours; new posts require groundworks, concrete setting times, and potentially traffic management.

## The Scoping Process

### 1. Review Initial Enquiry Documents

Examine all attachments for clues:
- **Purchase order descriptions** often use ambiguous terms like "tourist map" or "information board"
- **Size specifications** (e.g., "1200mm x 800mm") don't indicate whether this is panel-only or full unit
- **Location lists** may hint at scope—car parks and new developments often need full units; town centre replacements are often panel swaps

### 2. Ask Direct Clarifying Questions

Use this proven question template:

```
Before pricing, please confirm for each location:
1. Which require full new units (frame + posts + installation)?
2. Which are panel replacements into existing structures?
```

**Don't assume**—even experienced clients sometimes conflate the two or haven't fully specified their requirements.

### 3. Request Site Context

If answers are unclear, request:
- **Photos of existing sites** showing current structures and condition
- **Confirmation of attachment method** (free-standing posts vs. wall-mounted vs. attached to existing infrastructure)
- **Access details** that might indicate whether existing foundations are present

### 4. Document the Client's Response

In the Berwick example, the client clarified:
- Only **Location C (Parade Car Park)** attaches to existing structure = **panel replacement**
- Implied: Other locations = **new free-standing units**

Log this in Cairn immediately under the enquiry notes to ensure the quotation matches the confirmed scope.

## Common Scope Scenarios

| Client Says | Usually Means | Check For |
|-------------|---------------|-----------|
| "Replace the map" | Panel only | Condition of existing frame |
| "New tourist board" | Full unit | Site has no existing structure |
| "Update information" | Panel only | Artwork change vs. structural need |
| "Supply and install" | Ambiguous | **Always clarify** |

## Red Flags and Warnings

⚠️ **Mixed Scope Projects**: The Berwick enquiry demonstrates a common pattern—multiple locations with different requirements in a single PO. Create separate quote line items for each scope type.

⚠️ **"We'll provide artwork"**: This often indicates panel replacement thinking, but doesn't confirm it. Client may still expect full units at other locations.

⚠️ **Attachment to existing structures**: This phrase can mean:
- Panel replacement in existing NBNE frame ✓
- New panel attached to building/infrastructure (requires brackets, fixings, possibly planning permission)

⚠️ **Heritage/conservation areas**: Berwick-upon-Tweed is a historic town. New posts in such locations may require:
- Listed building consent
- Conservation area approval
- Specific colour/material specifications

Always flag potential planning requirements early.

## Quotation Structure for Mixed Projects

When confirmed scope includes both types:

```
SECTION A: Panel Replacements
- Location C (Parade Car Park): 1200 x 800mm panel to existing frame

SECTION B: New Display Units  
- Location A: [size] complete unit with posts
- Location B: [size] complete unit with posts

Installation: [separate rates for panel swap vs. new install]
```

This transparency helps clients understand cost differences and prevents scope creep.

## Follow-Up Best Practices

1. **Confirm in writing**: Email summary of agreed scope before quoting
2. **Site visit caveat**: "Quotation based on information provided; site survey may identify additional requirements"
3. **Planning assumption**: "Client responsible for obtaining necessary permissions unless otherwise agreed"
4. **Artwork compatibility**: Confirm file formats and production specs before manufacture
