# Quote for A Board/Pavement Sign

## Summary
Customer request for quotation on an A-board/pavement sign for the Serviceman Club in Alnmouth. The venue has transitioned from a private members club to a public house but public perception hasn't caught up—potential customers still believe membership is required. The sign aims to communicate that the venue is open to all, including families (children welcome until 9pm), and promote key offerings: affordable drinks, pool table, and darts board. Customer has a new board ready but is open to alternative suggestions.

## Key Customer Requirements

1. **Primary Objective**: Change public perception from "members only club" to "public house open to everyone"
2. **Target Audience**: General public including families with children
3. **Key Messages to Convey**:
   - No membership/sign-in required
   - Family-friendly (children until 9pm)
   - Competitive drink prices
   - Recreational facilities (pool and darts)
4. **Existing Asset**: Customer already has a new board

## Quote Preparation Steps

### 1. Clarify Board Specifications
Before providing quote, confirm with customer:
- Dimensions of existing board (if using their board)
- Material type (wooden frame, metal, plastic)
- Whether quote is for complete unit or graphics only
- Preferred finish (chalkboard, printed vinyl, inserted posters)

### 2. Assess Location Requirements
Request or verify:
- Pavement width and foot traffic patterns in Alnmouth
- Local council restrictions on A-board placement
- Weather exposure (coastal location—salt air considerations)
- Storage requirements (will it be brought in nightly?)

### 3. Recommend Appropriate Solution

**Option A: Graphics Only** (if using customer's board)
- Printed vinyl graphics applied to existing board
- Cost-effective
- Professional appearance
- Weather-resistant lamination essential for coastal location

**Option B: Complete A-Board Supply**
- Heavy-duty frame suitable for coastal winds
- Weighted base recommended for Alnmouth location
- Poster insert system for easy message updates
- Consider chalkboard option for daily specials

### 4. Message Design Consultation

Suggest concise, clear messaging:
- **Headline**: "OPEN TO ALL - NO MEMBERSHIP NEEDED"
- **Subheadings**: Family-friendly, Great value drinks, Pool & Darts
- **Operating info**: Opening hours, children welcome until 9pm
- Recommend professional design service if needed (add to quote)

### 5. Quote Structure

Include in quotation:
- **Line Item 1**: Board supply (if applicable) with specifications
- **Line Item 2**: Graphics/vinyl application with design
- **Line Item 3**: Delivery/installation to Alnmouth
- **Optional Add-ons**: 
  - Spare poster inserts
  - Protective coating for coastal conditions
  - Design services
- Payment terms and lead time
- Validity period of quote

## Warnings and Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Coastal Weather Considerations**: Alnmouth is exposed to coastal conditions. Standard boards may not withstand salt air and wind—recommend marine-grade materials or protective coatings.

⚠️ **Local Authority Compliance**: Many councils require permission for A-boards on public pavements. Customer should verify with Northumberland County Council before purchase. Non-compliance can result in confiscation.

⚠️ **Pavement Obstruction**: Board must not impede accessibility. Minimum 1.5m clear passage typically required for wheelchair/pushchair access.

⚠️ **Licensing Display Requirements**: As licensed premises, certain statutory notices may need to be visible. Confirm the board won't obscure required signage.

⚠️ **Message Clarity**: Avoid cluttering the sign with too much information. The key message ("OPEN TO ALL") should be readable from 10+ meters away.

## Follow-Up Actions

1. Send quote within 24-48 hours
2. Include visual mockup if possible (increases conversion)
3. Offer site visit if significant order value justifies it
4. Provide samples of materials/finishes if customer uncertain
5. Set reminder to follow up if no response within 1 week

## Additional Recommendations for Customer

Consider suggesting complementary solutions:
- Window graphics reinforcing "Open to Public" message
- Social media support to spread awareness of new public status
- Directional signage if venue is off main thoroughfare
- Interior signage welcoming first-time visitors
