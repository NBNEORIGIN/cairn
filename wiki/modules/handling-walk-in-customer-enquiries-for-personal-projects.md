# Handling Walk-In Customer Enquiries for Personal Projects

This article covers how to handle enquiries from members of the public seeking to use NBNE facilities for personal or school projects. These enquiries occasionally come through sales@ but fall outside our core business model. The example case involves a GCSE student project requiring acrylic cutting.

## Summary

Personal project enquiries (school projects, hobby work, one-off commissions) require professional handling even when they don't align with NBNE's commercial focus. While we maintain positive relationships with potential future customers, these jobs are typically unprofitable due to setup time, material minimums, and technical support requirements. This article outlines how to assess, quote, and appropriately redirect such enquiries.

## Identifying Personal Project Enquiries

**Common indicators:**
1. Student projects (GCSE, A-Level, university coursework)
2. Hobby or craft projects
3. Single units or very small quantities
4. Customer supplying own design files (.dxf, .ai, .svg)
5. Requests to "discuss materials" or seek general advice
6. Delivery via memory stick rather than professional file transfer
7. Email addresses from personal domains (btinternet.com, gmail.com, etc.)

## Initial Response Protocol

**Step 1: Acknowledge promptly and professionally**
- Respond within 24 hours to maintain good customer relations
- Thank them for their enquiry
- Confirm receipt of any files

**Step 2: Assess commercial viability**
- Quantity required (single units rarely viable)
- Material requirements (do we stock suitable materials in appropriate sizes?)
- Complexity of cutting/fabrication
- Timeline expectations
- Technical support likely needed

**Step 3: Provide realistic expectations**
- Minimum charges (setup time, material minimums)
- Lead times (may be 2-3 weeks for small jobs)
- Material costs (full sheet charges may apply even for small pieces)

## Consultation Meetings

If a face-to-face meeting is arranged (as in the Kit's Project example):

**Do:**
- Allocate a specific time slot
- Review files beforehand if possible
- Prepare material samples and pricing
- Be honest about costs and viability
- Suggest alternatives if we cannot help

**Don't:**
- Spend excessive time on technical advice for non-viable jobs
- Make promises about pricing without full assessment
- Commit to unrealistic timelines
- Provide free design consultancy

⚠️ **Warning:** Time spent on consultations for small jobs directly impacts profitability. Limit initial meetings to 15-20 minutes unless a deposit is taken.

## Quoting Personal Projects

**Minimum charge structure:**
- Setup fee: Covers file preparation, machine setup, material handling
- Material cost: Often full or half-sheet minimum, not just material used
- Cutting time: Machine hourly rate
- Finishing/delivery if applicable

**Typical minimum viable charge: £80-120** for simple acrylic cutting jobs.

Most personal project customers experience "sticker shock" at commercial pricing. Be prepared to explain why costs differ from online laser-cutting services.

## When to Decline

**Politely decline if:**
- Project value under £100 with high setup requirements
- Extensive design support needed
- Unrealistic timeline (needed tomorrow/next day)
- Customer unable to provide suitable files
- Materials unavailable or unsuitable for our equipment
- Liability concerns (toys, safety equipment, structural components)

## Alternative Referrals

Maintain a list of suitable alternatives:
- Local FabLabs or Makerspaces (often have public access laser cutters)
- Online cutting services (Razorlab, Lasered, etc.)
- College/university workshop facilities
- Hobbyist laser cutting services

Providing these referrals maintains goodwill even when declining work.

## Follow-up Management

As shown in the example, circumstances change (customer returning to Cornwall). When customers withdraw:
- Acknowledge professionally
- Keep door open for future commercial opportunities
- File contact details appropriately

⚠️ **Common Pitfall:** Spending excessive time helping personal project customers when production work is waiting. Prioritize commercial customers.

## Record Keeping

Even for declined enquiries:
- Log in CRM/Deek with "Personal Project - Declined" tag
- Note reason for declining
- Record any follow-up actions
- Keep file examples if illustrative of common requests
