# Wanted: Print Equipment Buyers

## Summary

External buyers actively seek used print equipment, wide format machines, finishing equipment, and surplus consumables (toners, inks, drums) from NBNE operations. Companies like Uniprint offer on-the-spot payment and collection services, with particular interest in Xerox, Konica Minolta (KM), and HP equipment. Understanding how to handle these inquiries properly ensures compliance with asset management policies while potentially recovering value from surplus equipment.

## How to Handle Equipment Buyer Inquiries

### 1. Do Not Respond Directly

**WARNING:** Never engage directly with external equipment buyers or commit to selling any NBNE assets without proper authorization. All equipment, even if obsolete or surplus, remains company property until formally disposed of through approved channels.

### 2. Forward to Asset Management

Route all equipment buyer inquiries to your Asset Management team or designated contact immediately. Include:
- Original email or contact details
- Any specific equipment mentioned
- Timeline mentioned (e.g., "in your area next week")
- Buyer's contact information

### 3. Identify Potentially Surplus Equipment

If the inquiry prompts you to recognize surplus equipment in your location:
- Create an inventory list of unused/obsolete items
- Note equipment type, manufacturer, model numbers
- Document condition (working, non-working, parts only)
- Include consumables that are expired or incompatible with current fleet
- Photograph items if possible

### 4. Follow Internal Disposal Procedures

NBNE maintains specific protocols for equipment disposal:
- Submit surplus equipment reports through your standard asset management system
- Await authorization before moving or segregating equipment
- Equipment may be reassigned to other NBNE locations before external sale
- Some items may require data sanitization or hard drive removal before disposal

## Common Equipment Categories Buyers Seek

### Print Hardware
- Copiers and multifunction devices
- Wide format printers and plotters
- Finishing equipment (folders, binders, laminators)
- Production print equipment

### Consumables
- Toner cartridges (especially Xerox, KM, HP)
- Ink cartridges
- Drums and imaging units
- Unopened or surplus stock

### Priority Brands
Buyers typically prioritize:
- **Xerox** - High demand for all models
- **Konica Minolta (KM)** - Strong secondary market
- **HP** - Popular for consumables and smaller devices

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Unauthorized Sales**: Selling company equipment without authorization can result in disciplinary action and may constitute theft of company property.

⚠️ **Data Security Risks**: Print devices contain hard drives with potentially sensitive client information. Never allow removal without IT security clearance.

⚠️ **Contractual Obligations**: Some equipment may be under lease or service contracts. Disposal may breach agreements or incur penalties.

⚠️ **Tax and Accounting Issues**: Asset disposal must be properly documented for financial reporting and tax purposes.

⚠️ **Consumables Inventory**: Surplus consumables may be better redistributed internally rather than sold, especially if compatible with equipment at other locations.

## When Equipment Buyers Are Legitimate

Asset Management may choose to engage buyers like Uniprint when:
- Equipment is confirmed surplus to requirements
- Internal redistribution options are exhausted
- Proper disposal protocols have been completed
- Quotes have been obtained and approved
- Data security requirements are met

Benefits of authorized sales include:
- Immediate payment and collection
- No disposal costs
- Environmental responsibility (equipment reuse)
- Recovery of some asset value

## Documentation Requirements

If Asset Management approves engagement with an equipment buyer, ensure:
1. Written authorization from Asset Management
2. Asset tags recorded and reported
3. Data sanitization certificates (for devices with storage)
4. Collection documentation signed
5. Payment receipt confirmation
6. Asset register updated to reflect disposal
