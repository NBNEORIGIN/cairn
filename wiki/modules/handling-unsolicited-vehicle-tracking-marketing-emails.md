# Handling Unsolicited Vehicle Tracking Marketing Emails

## Summary

NBNE operators may occasionally receive unsolicited marketing emails promoting third-party vehicle tracking systems or fleet management services. These emails are typically spam or phishing attempts and should be deleted without engagement. NBNE has established fleet management protocols and approved vendor relationships. This article explains how to identify these emails, proper handling procedures, and escalation paths if needed.

## Identifying Unsolicited Fleet Technology Marketing

### Common Characteristics

1. **Generic subject lines** mentioning "upgrades," "new technology," or future dates (e.g., "Upgrade NBNE's Vehicle Tracking in 2026")
2. **Vague body content** with minimal specific information about NBNE's actual fleet or operations
3. **Tracking URLs** with long, obfuscated links (often containing strings like "track.expertmarket.com")
4. **Regional selector forms** asking you to identify your location to "find suppliers"
5. **Unsolicited nature** - emails arrive without prior contact or requests for information
6. **Non-NBNE sender addresses** from marketing platforms or lead generation services

### Red Flags

**⚠️ WARNING:** Do not click links in these emails. The tracking URLs can:
- Confirm your email address is active, leading to more spam
- Redirect to lead generation forms that sell your contact information
- Potentially contain malicious content or phishing attempts

## Proper Handling Procedure

### Step 1: Initial Assessment

When you receive an email about fleet management, vehicle tracking, or similar services:

1. Check the sender's email address - is it from an approved NBNE vendor or internal contact?
2. Verify if you or your department requested information about fleet services
3. Look for the warning signs listed above

### Step 2: Do Not Engage

If the email is unsolicited:

- **Do not click** any links or buttons
- **Do not reply** to the sender
- **Do not forward** the email to colleagues (except IT Security if reporting)
- **Do not fill out** any forms or surveys

### Step 3: Report and Delete

1. Mark the email as spam/junk in your email client
2. If the email appears to be a phishing attempt (requests credentials, urgent action, or seems suspicious), forward it to IT Security before deleting
3. Delete the email from your inbox and trash folder

## NBNE Fleet Management Protocols

### Approved Processes

**📌 IMPORTANT:** NBNE has established procedures for fleet management decisions:

- Fleet technology evaluations are conducted by the Fleet Operations team in coordination with Procurement
- Vehicle tracking systems are standardized across the organization
- Only pre-approved vendors are used for fleet services
- Individual operators should not evaluate or procure fleet management solutions independently

### If You Have Legitimate Fleet Concerns

If you have actual concerns about vehicle tracking or fleet management:

1. Contact your Fleet Operations supervisor
2. Submit suggestions through the proper internal channels (Cairn suggestion portal or team meetings)
3. Discuss with your line manager during regular check-ins

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Assuming It's Official Communication

The subject line may reference "NBNE's fleet" or use company-specific language, but this doesn't make it legitimate. Marketing services scrape company information from public sources.

### Pitfall 2: "Just Looking" at Offers

Even viewing these offers by clicking links can compromise your contact information and lead to persistent follow-up from sales teams.

### Pitfall 3: Forwarding to "Someone Who Might Be Interested"

This spreads the spam internally and may expose colleagues to the same risks. Use proper channels if you believe NBNE should evaluate new fleet technology.

### Pitfall 4: Unsubscribing

Unsubscribe links in unsolicited emails often just confirm your email address is monitored. Only use unsubscribe features from legitimate senders you previously engaged with.
