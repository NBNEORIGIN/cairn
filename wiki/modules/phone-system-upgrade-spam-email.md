# Phone System Upgrade Spam Email

## Summary

This is a **spam/phishing email** disguised as a business telephone system upgrade offer. The email falsely claims NBNE's phones are "due for an upgrade" and attempts to redirect recipients to third-party sales comparison sites. This is a common B2B lead generation scam that harvests company information and bombards users with unwanted sales calls. **Delete immediately and do not click any links.**

## Identification Markers

### 1. Sender Characteristics
- Generic subject line with recipient name ("Toby Fletcher, do you have the best Telephone System...")
- Uses urgency tactics ("due for an upgrade")
- Sender domain is likely not from a legitimate NBNE vendor or IT department
- Contains tracking URLs (expertmarket.com tracking links)

### 2. Content Red Flags
- **No legitimate vendor relationship**: The email doesn't reference any existing NBNE supplier or contract
- **Generic messaging**: "More and more, our customers are coming to us..." - deliberately vague
- **Suspicious timing**: Claims phones are "due" for upgrade without any actual procurement cycle information
- **Lead generation funnel**: Multiple business type options (Sole Trader, LTD, Public Sector) designed to capture maximum leads

### 3. Technical Indicators
- Obfuscated tracking URLs with long encoded strings
- Multiple identical URLs suggesting automated template generation
- External redirect links rather than direct vendor contact

## Why This Is Dangerous

1. **Data harvesting**: Clicking links or filling forms provides your contact details to third-party lead aggregators
2. **Sales bombardment**: Results in persistent unwanted calls from multiple telephone system vendors
3. **Information disclosure**: May inadvertently reveal details about NBNE's infrastructure and procurement needs
4. **Phishing risk**: Could lead to more sophisticated targeted attacks once scammers confirm active email addresses

## Correct Response Protocol

### Immediate Actions
1. **Delete the email** without clicking any links
2. **Do not forward** to colleagues asking "is this legitimate?" (spreads the email further)
3. **Report as spam/phishing** using your email client's reporting function
4. **Alert IT Security** if you accidentally clicked any links

### If Links Were Clicked
1. Clear browser cache and cookies immediately
2. Report to NBNE IT Security team
3. Monitor for increased spam/sales calls in following weeks
4. Do **not** provide any business information on linked websites

## Legitimate Procurement Process

For reference, **actual** NBNE phone system upgrades would follow these channels:

- Initiated by IT/Facilities department through proper change management
- Communicated via internal channels (Cairn, official NBNE email domain)
- Include specific project codes, timelines, and named internal contacts
- Never solicit information via external marketing links
- Include proper procurement and approval workflows

## Common Variants

Similar spam campaigns targeting NBNE may claim:
- Broadband/internet services need upgrading
- Energy contracts are expiring
- Insurance policies require renewal
- Office supplies are on special offer
- IT equipment leases are ending

**All follow the same pattern**: create false urgency + redirect to lead generation sites.

## Prevention Tips

- **Verify sender domain carefully** - legitimate vendors use consistent, verifiable domains
- **Check for personalization depth** - real vendor emails reference specific account numbers, contract dates, or previous correspondence
- **Be suspicious of unsolicited "deadlines"** - scammers manufacture urgency
- **Hover over links** (don't click) to preview actual destination URLs
- **When in doubt, contact the supposed sender directly** using contact details from NBNE's approved vendor list, not from the email
