# GS1 UK Planned Service Downtime

GS1 UK is implementing a major system upgrade to introduce QR code generation capabilities within My Numberbank and My GS1. This will result in a planned service outage from **Friday 17 April 5:00pm (BST) to Monday 20 April 2:00pm (BST)**. During this period, users will be unable to access My GS1 and My Numberbank, and no payments can be processed. This article provides guidance for NBNE operators to prepare for and manage this disruption.

## Service Downtime Details

**Affected Services:**
- My Numberbank (complete access suspension)
- My GS1 (complete access suspension)
- Payment processing systems

**Downtime Window:**
- **Start:** Friday 17 April, 5:00pm BST
- **End:** Monday 20 April, 2:00pm BST
- **Duration:** Approximately 69 hours (full weekend)

## What's Changing

GS1 UK is deploying a new "QR codes powered by GS1" capability that will allow users to:
- Generate QR codes alongside traditional barcodes
- Access Premium QR functionality with additional features
- Future-proof packaging for evolving retailer and regulatory requirements
- Unlock additional value from product identifiers

This represents a significant platform upgrade affecting both My Numberbank and My GS1 backend systems.

## Pre-Downtime Preparation Checklist

1. **Audit Current Requirements**
   - Review any projects requiring new GTINs or barcodes before 17 April
   - Check upcoming product launches or packaging changes scheduled during the downtime window

2. **Generate Required Numbers and Barcodes**
   - Request all necessary GTINs, barcodes, and certificates **before Friday 17 April, 5:00pm BST**
   - Download and store barcode files locally
   - Print or save any required documentation

3. **Communicate with Stakeholders**
   - Notify internal teams (Product, Marketing, Design) of the service blackout
   - Alert any external partners who may need GS1 data during this period
   - Set expectations with suppliers regarding barcode provision delays

4. **Document Access Credentials**
   - Ensure you have correct login details for when services resume
   - Note any pending renewals or payments that might be affected

## ⚠️ Critical Warnings

**PAYMENT PROCESSING UNAVAILABLE:** No invoices can be paid during the downtime period. If your GS1 membership renewal or invoice payment is due during this window, arrange payment either before 17 April or plan for delays after 20 April.

**NO EMERGENCY ACCESS:** There will be no workaround or emergency access to generate barcodes during the outage. Plan accordingly for time-sensitive projects.

**POTENTIAL OVERRUN:** While services are scheduled to return by Monday 20 April at 2:00pm BST, system upgrades can experience delays. Build buffer time into critical project timelines.

**EXTERNAL VALIDATION SYSTEMS:** Third-party barcode validation tools or GS1 GTIN lookups may also be affected. Test alternative validation methods before the outage.

## During the Downtime

- Monitor official GS1 UK communications for status updates
- Do not attempt to create workarounds or temporary solutions that might violate GS1 standards
- Log any urgent requirements that arise so they can be processed immediately when services resume

## When Services Resume

1. **Verify Access**
   - Test login access to My Numberbank and My GS1
   - Confirm all previously generated barcodes are still accessible

2. **Process Queued Requests**
   - Submit any barcode or GTIN requests that accumulated during downtime
   - Complete any pending payments

3. **Explore New Functionality**
   - Familiarize yourself with the new QR code generation features
   - Review documentation for Premium QR capabilities
   - Assess whether QR codes could benefit your product lines

## Common Pitfalls

- **Last-minute requests:** Attempting to generate barcodes on Friday afternoon may result in missing the cutoff
- **Forgotten renewals:** Not checking membership renewal dates that fall during the outage window
- **Assuming partial access:** No aspect of My Numberbank or My GS1 will be available—plan for complete unavailability
- **Third-party dependencies:** Not considering that partners or suppliers may also be affected
