# Handling Multi-Item Quote Reduction Requests

## Summary

When a customer requests minimum pricing across multiple items after receiving a full quote, especially when comparing against historical quotes, the priority is to identify the most cost-effective solution while managing expectations about pricing currency. This article covers how to respond when customers ask to "select minimum quotes possible" across a product list, particularly when they're anchoring to older pricing.

## Understanding the Request

Amit is asking to cherry-pick the lowest possible price for each of 5 items and combine them into a single quote. This is a **comparison-based negotiation** where the customer has:

- Received a current quote (Quote 2) at £519.00 total
- Referenced an older quote with lower pricing on some items
- Now wants the absolute minimum across all five items bundled together

### What the Customer Actually Wants

The customer is not necessarily asking for a discount—they're asking you to **confirm which configuration offers the lowest total cost** for their specific needs. They may also be testing whether older pricing still applies.

## Response Strategy

### Step 1: Acknowledge Price Comparison Directly

Toby's response correctly acknowledged the historical quote and explained that current pricing reflects current costs. This prevents back-and-forth and sets realistic expectations.

**Template language:**
> "I have checked this against the earlier enquiry. Our current pricing reflects our present material and production costs, so there is only limited scope for adjustment."

### Step 2: Identify the Most Cost-Effective Current Options

From the current quote, Toby correctly identified:

- **2 × A1 PVC banners with eyelets: £50.00 + VAT** (lowest banner option)
- **50 × A6 thank you cards: £25.00 + VAT** (reduced quantity matches old quote)

This totals **£75.00 + VAT** for the minimum spec items.

### Step 3: Present Upgrade Paths Clearly

For items where the customer specified premium options (roller banners, desktop pull-ups), confirm:

- Whether they still need those items
- What the upgrade cost would be
- That the customer should confirm which route before proceeding

Toby offered the roller banners at £140.00 + VAT as an optional premium upgrade.

### Step 4: Handle the Missing Items

⚠️ **Warning:** Amit's list included 5 items, but Toby's response only addressed 2–3. The missing items were:

- A1 5mm Foam banner printing (customer specified this, but Toby offered PVC instead)
- A3 roller banners / Desktop pull-up banners (not priced in response)
- Delivery cost (not included in revised quote)

**Always address every line item explicitly**, even if you're recommending an alternative.

## Common Pitfalls

### ❌ Cherry-Picking From Old Quotes

Customers may try to combine the lowest historical price for each item. **Old quotes are not a menu.** Pricing changes due to:

- Material cost fluctuations
- Supplier pricing updates
- Seasonal or volume-based pricing that may have expired

### ❌ Assuming Product Substitutions Are Acceptable

Amit asked for **A1 5mm foam banners** but was offered **A1 PVC banners**. While PVC may be cheaper, the customer specified foam for a reason (rigidity, presentation quality). Always confirm substitutions are acceptable.

### ❌ Omitting Delivery Costs in Revised Quotes

The original quote included £15.00 delivery. If the customer is comparing total costs, omitting delivery creates confusion when invoicing.

### ❌ Not Clarifying Quantity Changes

Toby offered 50 cards instead of 110. This is a valid cost reduction, but it should be flagged clearly:
> "To match the earlier quote pricing, I've adjusted the quantity to 50 cards. If you need the full 110, the price would be [X]."

## Correct Response Template

When a customer asks for "minimum quotes possible" across multiple items:

1. **List each item with the minimum cost option:**
   - Item 1: [Product] - [Qty] - [Price]
   - Item 2: [Product] - [Qty] - [Price]
   
2. **Subtotal the minimum configuration**

3. **Offer upgrade options separately:**
   - "If you would prefer [premium option], the additional cost would be [X]"

4. **Add delivery and VAT clearly**

5. **Request confirmation before proceeding:**
   - "Please confirm which configuration you'd like to proceed with and I'll arrange the payment link."

## Next Steps After Customer Reply

Once the customer confirms their selection:

- Issue a **fresh quote document** with only the confirmed items
