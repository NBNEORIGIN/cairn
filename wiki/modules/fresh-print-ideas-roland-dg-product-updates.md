# Fresh Print Ideas - Roland DG Product Updates

Roland DG has highlighted four key developments for print businesses looking to expand services and improve workflows: direct-to-object UV printing (MO Series), integrated print-and-cut solutions (TrueVIS XG-640), award-winning DTF technology (TY-300i), and streamlined design software (Connect Designer). These solutions address common operational challenges including service diversification, production speed, and reducing dependency on external design resources.

## Key Product Developments

### 1. Direct-to-Object UV Printing (MO Series)

**Capability Overview:**
- Prints directly onto three-dimensional objects up to 204mm diameter
- Compatible materials: plastic, acrylic, wood, metal, glass, cardboard, leather
- UV curing technology for immediate durability

**Business Applications:**
- Personalised promotional items
- Custom branding on existing products
- Short-run decorative items
- Prototype decoration and sampling

**Operational Considerations:**
- Requires object fixturing and height calibration
- Material surface preparation affects adhesion
- UV ink handling procedures must be followed
- Consider ventilation requirements for UV curing process

### 2. Print-and-Cut Integration (TrueVIS XG-640)

**Performance Specifications:**
- Combined print and contour cut in single workflow
- Production speed: up to 15.2 m²/h
- Eliminates material handling between separate devices

**Workflow Benefits:**
- Reduces handling errors and material waste
- Maintains registration accuracy throughout process
- Decreases floor space requirements vs. separate units
- Improves turnaround times for cut graphics

**Production Planning:**
- Best suited for high-volume decal and label work
- Queue management becomes critical at higher throughputs
- Regular maintenance of both print and cut components required
- Consider RIP software capabilities for efficient nesting

### 3. DTF Technology (TY-300i)

**Award Recognition:**
- 2026 BLI Pick Award recipient
- Independent testing validated 20+ wash-and-dry cycle durability
- Recognised for speed and print quality balance

**DTF Process Advantages:**
- No pre-treatment of garments required
- Works on variety of fabric types and colours
- Film transfer allows inventory flexibility
- Suitable for complex, full-colour designs

**Quality Control Points:**
- Powder application consistency affects durability
- Curing temperature and time must be monitored
- Film storage conditions impact adhesive performance
- Test wash cycles before accepting large orders

### 4. Design Workflow Software (Roland DG Connect Designer)

**Primary Functions:**
- Streamlines file preparation for production
- Reduces design-to-production errors
- Decreases reliance on external design support
- Accelerates job turnaround

**Implementation Benefits:**
- Operators can handle routine design adjustments in-house
- Standardised templates improve consistency
- File validation catches errors before production
- Reduces back-and-forth with customers on technical specifications

**Training Requirements:**
- Staff need basic design software familiarity
- Understanding of file formats and resolution requirements
- Knowledge of production constraints (bleed, safe zones, colour modes)
- Regular software updates may require process adjustments

## Strategic Considerations

### Service Expansion Planning
When considering these technologies:
- Evaluate current customer demand vs. speculative markets
- Calculate ROI based on realistic production volumes
- Factor in training time and learning curve productivity losses
- Consider consumable costs and supplier reliability

### Integration with Existing Operations
- Assess workflow compatibility with current systems
- Plan physical layout for material flow efficiency
- Review power and environmental requirements
- Ensure adequate technical support availability

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Over-Diversification:** Adding multiple new technologies simultaneously strains training resources and cash flow. Prioritise based on confirmed market demand.

⚠️ **Consumable Costs:** Initial equipment pricing doesn't reflect ongoing ink, film, powder, or substrate costs. Model complete cost-per-unit before committing.

⚠️ **Maintenance Schedules:** UV and DTF systems require strict maintenance adherence. Skipped maintenance leads to expensive downtime and quality issues.

⚠️ **File Preparation Bottlenecks:** Design software only helps if operators are trained. Budget time for proper training and development of standard operating procedures.
