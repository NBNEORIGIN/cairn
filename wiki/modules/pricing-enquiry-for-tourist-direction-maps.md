# Pricing Enquiry for Tourist Direction Maps

## Summary
This enquiry from Berwick-upon-Tweed Town Council requests a quotation for supply and installation of tourist direction maps. The customer will provide artwork, and has attached specifications including sizes and requirements. This is a straightforward pricing request for external signage requiring assessment of materials, installation location, and any site-specific considerations for a coastal town environment.

## Response Process

### 1. Review Attached Specifications
- Check the attachment for exact dimensions, quantities, and mounting requirements
- Identify material specifications (if stated) or determine appropriate recommendations
- Note any specific location details or installation constraints
- Document all requirements clearly before proceeding

### 2. Assess Project Requirements

**Material Selection:**
- Tourist maps require weather-resistant materials suitable for outdoor, coastal environments
- Berwick-upon-Tweed's coastal location means high exposure to salt air, wind, and moisture
- Consider vandal-resistant options appropriate for public spaces
- Standard options: aluminium composite panels, toughened glass, or acrylic with UV-resistant printing

**Installation Considerations:**
- Confirm whether installation is on existing posts/structures or requires new mountings
- Public realm installations may require permissions or coordination with highways
- Access requirements for installation teams
- Ground conditions if posts need to be installed

### 3. Information to Gather

Before providing a quotation, clarify:
- Exact installation locations (addresses or map references)
- Existing mounting infrastructure or requirement for new posts
- Any access restrictions or working hour limitations
- Required completion timeline
- Whether planning permission is already secured
- Preferred material if not specified in attachment

### 4. Pricing Structure

Include in your quotation:
- Material costs per unit (based on specification)
- Printing costs (customer artwork to be supplied print-ready)
- Installation labour and equipment
- Travel to Berwick-upon-Tweed (significant distance from most NBNE locations)
- Post/mounting hardware if required
- Site survey costs if necessary
- Any permits or traffic management if installing in public highways
- Breakdown per unit and total project cost

### 5. Artwork Requirements

Specify to the customer:
- Required file format (typically high-resolution PDF or vector files)
- Minimum resolution/DPI requirements
- Colour profile specifications
- Lead time needed after artwork approval
- Proof approval process

## Warnings and Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Customer-Supplied Artwork Risks**
- Always review artwork before committing to timelines
- Build in time for artwork amendments if files are not print-ready
- Clearly state in quotation that pricing assumes print-ready artwork
- Include terms for additional charges if artwork requires remedial work

⚠️ **Coastal Environment**
- Standard materials may have reduced lifespan in coastal conditions
- Recommend marine-grade fixings and materials
- Price appropriately for enhanced specifications

⚠️ **Public Sector Customer**
- May require formal quotation format
- Could be subject to procurement thresholds requiring multiple quotes
- Payment terms typically 30 days or longer
- May need public liability insurance details

⚠️ **Installation Location**
- Tourist maps often installed in high-footfall heritage areas
- May require listed building consent or conservation area approval
- Confirm responsibility for obtaining permissions
- Check if installation requires traffic management or parking suspensions

## Follow-Up Actions

1. Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
2. Review attachment and confirm all specifications are clear
3. Conduct site survey if locations are complex (charge appropriately)
4. Provide detailed quotation within 5-7 working days
5. Include validity period (typically 30 days)
6. Offer to discuss requirements by phone if needed
