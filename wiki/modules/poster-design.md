# Poster Design

## Summary
This article covers the poster design process at NBNE, from initial consultation through to client approval. The example demonstrates a successful design turnaround where multiple options were presented to the client (Rachel), resulting in quick approval of option 2. This workflow showcases best practices for client communication, design presentation, and managing approval timelines.

## Design Process Steps

### 1. Initial Client Consultation
- Gather all requirements from the client including dimensions, usage, branding guidelines, and any specific messaging
- Confirm deadline expectations and number of design iterations included
- Document any reference materials or previous designs the client wants to build upon

### 2. Prepare Multiple Design Options
Create 2-3 distinct design concepts rather than a single option. This approach:
- Gives clients choice and demonstrates creative range
- Increases likelihood of first-round approval
- Helps identify client preferences for future projects
- Shows investment in their project

**Best Practice:** Number your design options clearly (e.g., "Option 1", "Option 2", "Design 2") to make client feedback unambiguous.

### 3. Present Designs Professionally
When sending design options:
- Use clear subject lines that reference the project type
- Keep email copy concise and friendly
- Ensure all design files are properly formatted and easy to view
- Request specific feedback: "let me know what you think"
- Include your professional email signature

### 4. Manage Client Approval
- Respond promptly to client feedback
- Confirm which design option has been selected
- Document the approval in your project management system
- Progress immediately to production or next steps

## Email Communication Guidelines

### Professional Tone
Maintain a balance between professional and personable communication. The example shows:
- Polite greeting ("Hi Rachel")
- Clear purpose statement
- Friendly but professional sign-off
- Use of company email address (sales@nbnesigns.co.uk)

### Response Management
When receiving client approval:
- Acknowledge their choice and confirm next steps
- Note any holiday or out-of-office messages from clients
- Update project status in your tracking system
- Ensure handover notes are clear if you'll be away

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Warning: Presenting Too Many Options**
More than 3-4 design options can overwhelm clients and delay decision-making. Two to three well-considered options is optimal.

⚠️ **Warning: Ambiguous Design Labelling**
Always number or clearly label designs. Clients saying "I prefer the second one" can be ambiguous if designs aren't explicitly numbered.

⚠️ **Warning: Unclear Approval**
Ensure you have explicit approval before proceeding to production. Phrases like "I love number 2" constitute clear approval, but vague positive comments may require confirmation.

⚠️ **Warning: File Format Issues**
Always confirm clients can open and properly view your design files before they provide feedback. Consider sending PDFs or JPEGs as well as native design files.

⚠️ **Warning: Timeline Communication**
Note when clients mention holidays or time away. This example shows Rachel mentioning holiday, which could impact any required revisions or follow-up questions.

## Design Approval Tracking

### Documentation Requirements
For each poster design project, record:
- Client name and contact details
- Project reference number
- Design options presented (save all versions)
- Which option was approved
- Date of approval
- Any revision requests

### File Management
- Save all design iterations with clear version numbers
- Store approved designs in the client's project folder
- Maintain both editable source files and print-ready exports
- Archive correspondence including approval emails

## Production Handover

Once design approval is received:
1. Confirm final specifications (size, material, quantity)
2. Prepare print-ready files according to production requirements
3. Complete pre-flight checks
4. Submit to production with all relevant project notes
5. Update client on expected completion timeline
