# Fresh Print Ideas for NBNE Operations

This marketing email from Roland DG Europe highlights four print technology developments relevant to expanding NBNE service capabilities: direct-to-object UV printing, integrated print-and-cut solutions, DTF (Direct-to-Film) technology, and workflow design tools. While positioned as promotional content, it identifies emerging industry trends worth evaluating for operational expansion or efficiency improvements.

## Equipment and Technologies Highlighted

### 1. Direct-to-Object UV Printing (Roland DG MO Series)

**Capability**: UV printing directly onto three-dimensional objects up to 204mm in diameter

**Supported Materials**:
- Plastic and acrylic
- Wood and metal
- Glass
- Cardboard
- Leather

**Operational Considerations**: This technology could expand NBNE's customization services beyond flat media. Evaluate whether customer demand justifies the investment and whether sufficient volume exists for promotional products, branded merchandise, or specialty applications.

### 2. Print-and-Cut Integration (TrueVIS XG-640)

**Capability**: Combined printing and cutting in a single workflow

**Performance**: Up to 15.2 m²/h production speed

**Efficiency Benefits**:
- Eliminates transfer between separate print and cut stations
- Reduces handling time and potential alignment errors
- Streamlines workflow for signage, decals, and labels

**Operational Note**: Single-process solutions reduce labor requirements but create a single point of failure. Assess whether integrated or separate systems better suit your redundancy requirements and production volume.

### 3. DTF Technology (TY-300i)

**Recognition**: 2026 BLI Pick Award winner

**Performance Metrics**:
- Durability through 20+ wash-and-dry cycles
- High speed and print quality (specific metrics not provided in source)

**Application**: Direct-to-Film printing for textile and apparel customization

**Market Context**: DTF has gained traction as an alternative to traditional heat transfer and direct-to-garment (DTG) printing. Evaluate local demand for custom apparel services before considering this technology.

### 4. Workflow Software (Roland DG Connect Designer)

**Purpose**: File preparation and production workflow optimization

**Claimed Benefits**:
- Reduced file preparation time
- Fewer errors in production handoff
- Decreased reliance on external design resources
- Faster job turnaround

**Integration Consideration**: Assess compatibility with existing NBNE design and production workflows before adoption. Proprietary workflow tools can create vendor lock-in.

## Evaluation Framework for New Print Technologies

When assessing technologies like those mentioned above, apply this structured approach:

1. **Demand Validation**: Survey existing customers and identify specific unfulfilled requests that new capabilities would address
2. **Volume Projection**: Estimate realistic utilization rates (aim for 60%+ equipment utilization to justify investment)
3. **Margin Analysis**: Calculate per-job costs including consumables, maintenance, and labor against realistic pricing
4. **Training Requirements**: Factor in operator learning curves and certification needs
5. **Service and Support**: Verify local technical support availability and typical response times
6. **Integration**: Assess compatibility with existing workflows, software, and space constraints

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls

- **Technology-First Thinking**: Don't acquire equipment before validating customer demand. "Build it and they will come" rarely works in print services
- **Vendor Marketing Claims**: Production speeds and capacities quoted in marketing materials typically represent optimal conditions, not real-world performance with job changeovers and setup time
- **Consumables Costs**: UV inks, specialty media, and proprietary consumables can dramatically affect per-job costs. Request detailed consumables pricing before committing
- **Maintenance Overhead**: Specialty equipment often requires more frequent maintenance than standard digital presses. Factor this into operational planning

## Strategic Considerations

This email reflects broader industry trends toward:
- **Service diversification** (expanding beyond traditional flat printing)
- **Workflow automation** (reducing manual touchpoints and potential errors)
- **In-house consolidation** (bringing more production stages internal)

Evaluate whether these trends align with NBNE's strategic direction and customer base. Not every trend requires immediate adoption.
