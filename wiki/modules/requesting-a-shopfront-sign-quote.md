# Requesting a Shopfront Sign Quote

This article covers the process of obtaining a quote for external signage from a sign manufacturer. Based on a typical enquiry for a shopfront sign, it outlines the information you need to provide, expected costs, and the ordering process to ensure you receive an accurate quote without unnecessary back-and-forth.

## Information Required for an Accurate Quote

To receive a formal quote rather than rough estimates, you must provide the following details:

1. **Exact dimensions** - Specify in metres or centimetres (e.g., 2.83m x 0.27m)
2. **Design files** - Highest resolution available, preferably:
   - EPS format
   - Vector files
   - If only low-resolution files available, factor in additional design costs
3. **Installation location** - Full address where sign will be mounted
4. **Access considerations** - Mention any potential access issues (height, restricted access, scaffolding requirements)
5. **Photo of installation site** - Shows mounting surface and helps identify fixing requirements
6. **Delivery requirements** - Whether delivery and installation are needed

> **Warning:** Long, thin signs require multiple fixing points for stability. Ensure the mounting surface can accommodate this before proceeding with the order.

## Typical Cost Breakdown

Based on a 2.83m x 0.27m external sign:

### Base Sign Cost
- **Material:** ACM (aluminium composite) with vinyl overlam for external use
- **Cost:** £180.00 approximately

### Design Work
- **Rate:** £40 per hour
- **Estimated time:** 1-2 hours if high-resolution artwork is not available
- **Total design cost:** £40-£80

### Delivery
- **Cost:** Variable, depends on distance from supplier location
- Request specific quote based on delivery address
- Example supplier location: Alnwick (adjust for your supplier)

### Installation
- **Cost range:** £75-£150
- Varies based on:
  - Site accessibility
  - Height of installation
  - Complexity of fixing points required
  - Equipment needed (ladders, scaffolding, cherry picker)

## Payment Terms

Standard payment structure:
1. **Deposit:** 25% upfront when accepting the quote
2. **Balance:** Remaining 75% due upon project completion

## Process Steps

### Step 1: Initial Enquiry
Send email with all required information listed above. Include:
- Dimensions
- Design files as attachments
- Location details
- Photo of installation site
- Confirmation of delivery/installation requirements

### Step 2: Review Rough Estimate
Supplier will provide rough costs based on initial information. This is NOT a formal quote.

### Step 3: Provide Additional Information
Respond promptly with any missing details requested by the supplier.

### Step 4: Receive Formal Quote
Once all information is provided, supplier will send formal quote including:
- Exact materials and specifications
- Itemised costs
- Timeline for completion
- Payment terms

### Step 5: Accept and Pay Deposit
If proceeding, formally accept the quote and arrange 25% deposit payment.

### Step 6: Completion and Final Payment
Pay remaining balance upon satisfactory completion of installation.

## Common Pitfalls

**Insufficient artwork resolution** - Low-resolution files (JPEG, PNG) may look acceptable on screen but will be pixelated at large scale. Always check if vector files are available before requesting a sign. Budget an additional 1-2 hours of design time if you only have raster images.

**Underestimating installation complexity** - A quote without site photos may result in unexpected installation costs. Access issues, working at height, or difficult mounting surfaces can significantly increase installation fees.

**Missing fixing point requirements** - Long, narrow signs are particularly vulnerable to wind and require more fixing points than compact signs. Ensure your mounting surface can accommodate multiple fixings.

**Location-dependent delivery costs** - Delivery costs can vary significantly based on distance from the supplier. Always specify the exact delivery location upfront.

## Third-Party Work Considerations

When requesting quotes for work on behalf of colleagues, clients, or their family members:

- Ensure you have all required information before making initial contact
- Clarify who will be the point of contact for queries
- Confirm payment responsibility and approval authority
- Set clear expectations about timeline and your involvement
